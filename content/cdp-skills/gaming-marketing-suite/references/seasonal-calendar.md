# Gaming Seasonal Marketing Calendar

Strategic calendar for mobile games, console games, and esports marketing. Covers game launches, seasonal content, platform-driven events, and player behavior seasonality.

## Q1 - January to March

### January - New Year, New Players

**Timing:** Jan 1-31 (Peak: Jan 1-15)

**Player Behavior:**
- New Year resolution gamers (fitness games, brain training)
- Holiday break burnout recovery
- Holiday gift device activations (peak new installs)

**Campaign Opportunities:**
- New player acquisition campaigns (peak device activations)
- Returning player bonuses (lapsed from December)
- Content updates to captivate new players
- Tutorial optimization (new player volume highest)
- Beginner-friendly events (low barrier to entry)

**Gaming Calendar Triggers:**
- Game launches (competing for attention)
- Platform sales ending (last wave of gift devices active)
- School returns (social gaming among peers)

**Key Metrics to Track:**
- D1 retention (reflect of tutorial quality)
- CPI (competition high, may spike)
- New player cohort size
- Tutorial completion rate

---

### February - Global Events (Lunar New Year, Valentine's)

**Timing:** Feb 1-28 (Peak: Lunar New Year varies by calendar, Valentine's Feb 10-14)

**Regional Opportunities:**

- **Valentine's Day (Global):** Couple-themed content, friendship events, gifting mechanics

- Social feature usage (gifting)
- Event participation rate

## Cross-Seasonal Patterns & Insights

### Year-Round Core Players
- DAU stable 10-20% of MAU
- Purchase consistently (monthly active payers)
- Event participation high

**Strategy:** VIP treatment, exclusive perks, community recognition, early access.

### Seasonal Surge Players
- Active during specific seasons (students on break, holiday gamers)
- Multi-month dormancy normal
- Reactivate during their season

**Strategy:** Re-engagement campaigns 1 week before expected season; seasonal content highlights.

### Event-Driven Players
- Activate for specific events (esports, limited cosmetics)
- Otherwise dormant
- High impulse spending on exclusive items

**Strategy:** Event notification campaigns; FOMO mechanics; early bird cosmetic access.

### Holiday Gift Cohorts
- Activate via new device (holiday gifts)
- Often new-to-gaming demographics
- Require gentle onboarding

**Strategy:** Tutorial optimization; beginner-friendly events; barrier reduction to monetization.

## Campaign Timing Rules of Thumb

1. **New Content Release:** Launch 1-2 weeks before expected high engagement (holiday, event, seasonal peak)
2. **Cosmetic Sales:** Limited-time exclusivity drives FOMO purchases; 2-week windows optimal
3. **Battle Pass Reset:** Align with season starts (January, April, July, October for quarterly) or major events
4. **Reactivation Campaigns:** 4-6 weeks before seasonal peaks (prepare for incoming surge)
5. **Platform Sales:** Plan features 2 weeks prior to ensure new cohorts hit high-engagement content
6. **Esports Tie-ins:** Launch cosmetics 1 week before tournament begins; extend 1 week past finals
