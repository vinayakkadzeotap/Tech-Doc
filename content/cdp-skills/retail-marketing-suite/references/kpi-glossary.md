# Retail KPI Glossary

Comprehensive reference for retail and e-commerce key performance indicators with definitions, formulas, benchmarks, and interpretation guidance.

## Revenue & Value Metrics

### Average Order Value (AOV)

**Definition:** Mean monetary value of each transaction.

**Formula:**
```
AOV = Total Revenue / Total Orders
```

**Example:** $50,000 revenue ÷ 1,000 orders = $50 AOV

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- E-commerce (general): $30-60
- Apparel: $45-75
- Home & garden: $60-120
- Luxury goods: $150-500+
- CPG (grocery): $20-35

**How to Improve:**
- Upsell higher-priced variants
- Cross-sell complementary products
- Bundle offers (save 15% when buy 2+)
- Tiered discounts (free shipping at $75+)
- Premium product positioning

**When it matters:** Revenue optimization, pricing strategy, promotional effectiveness

---

### Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV)

**Definition:** Total net profit expected from a customer over their entire relationship.

**Formula (Simple):**
```
CLTV = (Average Order Value × Purchase Frequency × Customer Lifespan) - Customer Acquisition Cost
```

**Formula (Advanced):**
```
CLTV = Σ(Revenue in period - Cost in period) / (1 + Discount Rate)^period
```

**Example:**
- AOV: $50
- Purchase frequency: 6x per year
- Average customer lifespan: 5 years
- CAC: $20
- CLTV = ($50 × 6 × 5) - $20 = $1,480

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- E-commerce: 3-5x CAC ratio
- Luxury retail: 8-15x CAC ratio
- CPG: 2-3x CAC ratio

**How to Improve:**
- Increase repeat purchase rate (loyalty programs)
- Extend customer lifespan (retention campaigns)
- Increase order frequency (email triggers)
- Reduce churn (reactivation programs)

**When it matters:** Long-term profitability, customer acquisition budget allocation, retention investment decisions

---

### Gross Margin

**Definition:** Revenue minus cost of goods sold (COGS), expressed as percentage.

**Formula:**
```
Gross Margin % = (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue × 100
```

**Example:** ($100,000 revenue - $40,000 COGS) / $100,000 = 60% margin

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Apparel: 50-70%
- Electronics: 20-40%
- Home goods: 40-60%
- Luxury: 60-80%
- CPG: 25-45%

**How to Improve:**
- Optimize product mix (shift to higher-margin items)
- Negotiate better supplier terms
- Reduce returns and product waste
- Pricing strategy optimization
- Reduce promotional discounting

**When it matters:** Profitability, sustainable scaling, investment in marketing

---

## Acquisition Metrics

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

**Definition:** Average cost to acquire one new customer.

**Formula:**
```
CAC = Total Acquisition Spending / New Customers Acquired
```

**Example:** $5,000 marketing spend ÷ 100 new customers = $50 CAC

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- E-commerce (general): $10-50
- Apparel DTC: $25-75
- High-AOV luxury: $50-150
- Subscription: $20-100

**How to Optimize:**
- Use lookalike audiences (lower CAC)
- Organic & referral programs (reduce paid CAC)
- Affiliate marketing (performance-based)
- Email list building (low CAC retention)
- SEO & content marketing (sustainable, low CAC)

**When it matters:** Marketing ROI, budget allocation, growth strategy, profitability timeline

---

### Conversion Rate

**Definition:** Percentage of visitors/traffic that result in a purchase.

**Formula:**
```
Conversion Rate % = Total Purchases / Total Visitors × 100
```

**Example:** 50 purchases ÷ 2,500 visitors = 2% conversion rate

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- E-commerce (general): 1-3%
- Apparel: 1.5-3.5%
- Luxury: 0.5-2%
- High-traffic (marketplace): 0.5-1.5%

**Segmentation (always analyze separately):**
- By traffic source: Organic higher (4-6%), paid lower (1-2%)
- By device: Desktop higher (2-3%), mobile lower (0.8-1.5%)
- By first-time vs. repeat: Repeat 3-5x higher

**How to Improve:**
- Site speed optimization (each 1-sec delay = -7% conversion)
- Clear product descriptions & images
- Reduce form friction
- Trust signals (reviews, guarantees, security badges)
- Mobile optimization

**When it matters:** Website effectiveness, traffic quality, profitability per visitor

---

## Retention & Loyalty Metrics

### Repeat Purchase Rate (RPR)

**Definition:** Percentage of customers who make more than one purchase.

**Formula:**
```
RPR % = Customers with 2+ purchases / Total customers × 100
```

**Example:** 250 repeat customers ÷ 1,000 total = 25% repeat rate

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- E-commerce (general): 20-35%
- Luxury: 15-25%
- CPG/Grocery: 60-80%
- DTC brands: 25-40%

**Time-Based Variants:**
- 30-day repeat rate: Purchases within 30 days of first purchase
- 90-day repeat rate: Purchases within 90 days
- Annual repeat rate: Purchases within 12 months

**How to Improve:**
- Post-purchase nurture (thank you email, care guides)
- Loyalty program incentives (points, tiers, exclusives)
- Personalized recommendations
- Win-back campaigns for lapsed customers
- Subscription or membership models

**When it matters:** Customer lifetime value, retention ROI, churn prediction, loyalty program design

---

### Cart Abandonment Rate

**Definition:** Percentage of shopping carts initiated but not completed.

**Formula:**
```
Abandonment Rate % = Abandoned Carts / Total Carts × 100
```

**Example:** 7,300 abandoned ÷ 10,000 total carts = 73% abandonment rate

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- E-commerce (general): 70-75%
- Apparel: 72-76%
- Luxury: 75-85%
- Grocery/CPG: 65-70%

**Recovery Opportunity:**
- 1st email (within 1 hour): 5-10% recovery
- 2nd email (24 hours): 3-5% recovery
- SMS (if available): 10-15% recovery
- Retargeted ads: 2-5% recovery

**Common Reasons & Solutions:**
- Unexpected costs (show total early, offer free shipping)
- Complicated checkout (1-page checkout, guest option)
- Payment issues (offer multiple payment methods)
- Security concerns (trust badges, SSL certificate visible)
- Not ready to buy (save for later, wishlist)

**When it matters:** Revenue recovery, checkout optimization, promotional efficiency

---

### Churn Rate

**Definition:** Percentage of customers lost in a period who don't purchase again.

**Formula:**
```
Churn Rate % = Lost Customers / Starting Customers × 100
```

**Example:** 50 lapsed customers ÷ 500 active customers = 10% monthly churn

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- E-commerce (general): 5-10% monthly
- Subscription: 2-5% monthly
- Luxury: 3-8% monthly
- CPG (repeat buyers): 15-20% monthly

**Critical Time Windows:**
- Month 2-3: High churn (didn't repeat quickly)
- Month 6+: Seasonal churn (holiday season dropoff)
- Year-over-year: Annual churn baseline

**How to Reduce:**
- Identify churn signals (purchase frequency decline, category dormancy)
- Reactivation campaigns (personalized offers, seasonal re-engagement)
- Win-back campaigns (lapsed 90+ days)
- Product/service improvements (reduce negative factors)
- Loyalty program (create stickiness)

**When it matters:** Retention strategy, reactivation budgeting, customer lifetime value modeling

---

## Transaction Metrics

### Basket Size

**Definition:** Average number of items per transaction.

**Formula:**
```
Basket Size = Total Items / Total Orders
```

**Example:** 5,000 items ÷ 2,500 orders = 2.0 items per basket

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- E-commerce (general): 1.5-2.5 items
- Apparel: 2-3 items
- Grocery/CPG: 10-20 items
- Luxury: 1-1.5 items (higher value items)

**Cross-Sell Performance:**
- 1-item baskets: 85% of baskets (no cross-sell success)
- 2-item baskets: 12% of baskets (successful cross-sell)
- 3+ item baskets: 3% of baskets (strong bundling)

**How to Increase:**
- Product recommendations ("Frequently bought together")
- Bundle offers (save when buy 2+)
- Tiered discounts (spend $50 = 10% off)
- Free shipping threshold (order $75+)
- Smart product placement (complementary items)

**When it matters:** AOV optimization, profitability per order, inventory management

## Quality & Performance Metrics

### Net Promoter Score (NPS)

**Definition:** Measure of customer satisfaction and likelihood to recommend (0-100 scale).

**Formula:**
```
NPS = % Promoters (9-10) - % Detractors (0-6)
Range: -100 to +100
```

**Example:**
- 50% gave 9-10 (promoters)
- 20% gave 0-6 (detractors)
- NPS = 50% - 20% = +30 NPS

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Excellent: +50 or higher
- Good: +30 to +49
- Average: 0 to +29
- Poor: Below 0

**Use for Customer Segmentation:**
- Promoters: Loyalty program, referral incentives, exclusive access
- Passives: Improve product/service, increase engagement
- Detractors: Win-back offers, feedback surveys, problem resolution

**When it matters:** Product-market fit, brand health, customer satisfaction, referral program ROI

---

### Return Rate

**Definition:** Percentage of orders returned for any reason.

**Formula:**
```
Return Rate % = Returned Orders / Total Orders × 100
```

**Example:** 150 returns ÷ 5,000 orders = 3% return rate

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Apparel: 25-30% (highest)
- Electronics: 5-10%
- Home goods: 5-8%
- Luxury: 3-5% (lower due to higher consideration)
- CPG/Grocery: <1%

**Return Reasons (typical breakdown):**
- Size/fit (apparel): 40%
- Wrong color/appearance: 25%
- Defective/damaged: 15%
- Changed mind: 15%
- Other (wrong order): 5%

**How to Reduce:**
- Better size guides & fit visualization
- Detailed product photos/videos
- Customer reviews (manage expectations)
- Quality assurance (reduce defects)
- Clear return policy (don't surprise customers)

**When it matters:** Gross margin, customer satisfaction, logistics planning, product development

---

### Sell-Through Rate

**Definition:** Percentage of inventory that sells within a period.

**Formula:**
```
Sell-Through % = Units Sold / Beginning Inventory × 100
```

**Example:** 400 units sold ÷ 1,000 units in stock = 40% sell-through

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Fast fashion: 70-90% per season
- Seasonal items: 60-80% before clearance
- Core items: 80-95% annual
- Clearance items: <20%

**Time-Based Targets:**
- End of season: 70-85% for normal margin
- 2 weeks into season: 20-30%
- End of year: 90%+

**How to Improve:**
- Demand forecasting (accurate inventory levels)
- Pricing optimization (higher for trending, lower for slow)
- Promotional timing (strategic discounts)
- Bundling (combine slow with fast movers)
- Product assortment (stock what sells)

**When it matters:** Inventory planning, markdown strategy, cash flow, storage costs

---

## Advanced Metrics

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Payback Period

**Definition:** Months to recoup CAC through gross profit.

**Formula:**
```
CAC Payback = CAC / (Monthly ARPU × Gross Margin %)
```

**Example:**
- CAC: $50
- Monthly ARPU: $20
- Gross margin: 60%
- CAC Payback = $50 / ($20 × 0.60) = 4.2 months

**Benchmarks:**
- Healthy: <12 months
- Aggressive: <6 months
- Unsustainable: >24 months

**When it matters:** Growth sustainability, unit economics, investment decisions

---

### Return on Advertising Spend (ROAS)

**Definition:** Revenue generated per dollar spent on advertising.

**Formula:**
```
ROAS = Revenue Attributed to Ads / Ad Spend
(2:1 ROAS = $2 revenue per $1 spent)
```

**Example:** $10,000 revenue ÷ $2,000 ad spend = 5:1 ROAS

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Paid search: 3:1 to 5:1
- Display/retargeting: 2:1 to 3:1
- Social media: 2:1 to 4:1
- Email marketing: 40:1 to 50:1 (highest)
- Affiliate: 3:1 to 5:1

**Channel-Specific ROAS:**
- New customer acquisition: Lower ROAS (2:1 to 3:1)
- Repeat customer campaigns: Higher ROAS (5:1 to 10:1+)
- Email to repeat customers: 20:1+

**When it matters:** Budget allocation, channel selection, promotional effectiveness

---

### Inventory Turnover

**Definition:** How many times inventory sells and is replaced in a period (typically annual).

**Formula:**
```
Inventory Turnover = COGS / Average Inventory Value
```

**Example:** $500,000 COGS ÷ $100,000 avg inventory = 5 turns/year

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Fast fashion: 8-12 turns/year
- Traditional retail: 3-5 turns/year
- Luxury: 2-3 turns/year
- Seasonal goods: 1-2 turns/season

**Impact on Business:**
- High turnover: Low storage costs, fresh inventory, better cash flow
- Low turnover: High storage costs, risk of obsolescence, slow cash flow

**When it matters:** Cash flow management, storage optimization, seasonal planning
