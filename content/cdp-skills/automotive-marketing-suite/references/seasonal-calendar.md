# Automotive Seasonal Calendar

Strategic calendar for automotive OEM, dealership, and EV marketing campaigns aligned with model launches, buying seasons, and vehicle ownership patterns.

## Q1 (January - March)

### January
- **New Year Resolutions**: Vehicle upgrade resolutions, "new car smell" mentality
- **Tax Refund Season** (US): Buyers with refund windfalls available for down payments
- **Year-End Inventory Clearance**: Last chance deals on previous year models
- **CES/Tech Shows**: New technology features, autonomous driving announcements
- **Winter Driving Season**: 4WD, winter tires, cold-weather vehicle performance

**Campaign Focus**: Tax refund financing, year-end clearance, winter capability messaging

### February
- **Presidents Day/Family Day Weekend** (US/Canada): Long weekend test drive traffic
- **EV Incentive Windows**: Federal/state tax credits available to buyers
- **Valentine's Day**: Luxury, sports car positioning, couple/family vehicles
- **Winter Sports Season**: Vehicle performance content (snow, ice, terrain)
- **Tax Planning**: Buyers finalizing year-end vehicle purchases for tax benefits

**Campaign Focus**: Weekend test drive events, luxury/sports positioning, EV incentives

### March
- **Spring Break Prep**: Family vehicles, cargo capacity, road trip readiness
- **Daylight Saving Time**: Psychological trigger for vehicle upgrade/refresh
- **End of Q1 Model Clearance**: Remaining year-old inventory heavily discounted
- **Spring Maintenance**: Pre-spring vehicle service campaigns
- **Easter Holiday** (if in March): Family vehicle positioning

**Campaign Focus**: Family vehicle features, spring maintenance, road trip readiness, deals

## Q2 (April - June)

### April
- **Spring Buying Season Begins**: Optimal weather for test drives and outdoor dealership events
- **Earth Day**: EV positioning, emissions, sustainability messaging
- **Tax Deadline** (US): Post-filing financial readiness for vehicle purchases
- **Spring Vehicle Launches**: OEMs introduce refreshed/updated models
- **Spring Incentives**: Manufacturers increase incentives for older inventory

**Campaign Focus**: Spring launches, EV sustainability, family vehicles, spring incentives

### May
- **Memorial Day Weekend** (US): Long weekend purchasing and test drive events
- **Bank Holidays** (EU/UK): Extended weekends drive dealership traffic
- **End of School Year**: Family vacation vehicles, upgrade before summer road trips
- **Summer Planning Peak**: Buyers plan summer road trips and need capable vehicles
- **Convertible/Sports Car Season**: Top-down weather drives premium vehicle interest

**Campaign Focus**: Long weekend events, summer road trip vehicles, convertible/sports cars

### June
- **Summer Solstice**: Peak daylight, outdoor dealership events optimal
- **Father's Day**: Truck, SUV, sports car positioning (male-skewed vehicles)
- **School Summer Break**: Family vehicles peak demand
- **Summer Road Trip Season Opens**: Cargo, towing, fuel efficiency emphasis
- **Mid-Year Clearance**: Dealers push inventory to make Q2 sales targets

**Campaign Focus**: Father's Day gift vehicles, road trip prep, family SUVs, mid-year deals

## Q3 (July - September)

### July
- **Peak Summer Travel Season**: Vehicle usage peaks, service scheduling important
- **Summer Incentives**: Hot weather, vacation driving increases interest
- **Summer Classics**: Convertibles and sports cars at peak interest
- **Road Trip Content**: Driving guides, vehicle preparation, travel content
- **Family Vehicles**: Peak demand for accommodating larger vehicles

**Campaign Focus**: Summer road trip content, convertibles/sports cars, family vehicles

### August
- **Back-to-School Preparation**: Family vehicles for school runs, multiple children
- **New Model Year Arrives**: Current year models released in August
- **August Incentives**: Sales events and clearance for model-year turnover
- **End-of-Summer Deals**: Aggressive discounting to clear previous model year stock
- **University Orientation**: Young adults buying first vehicles for college

**Campaign Focus**: Back-to-school vehicles, new model year launch, student vehicles, end-of-summer deals

### September
- **New Model Year Launch**: Major model refreshes and redesigns introduced
- **Labor Day Weekend** (US): Final summer long weekend sales event
- **New School Year**: Vehicle needs shift from summer to school/activities focus
- **Fall Equinox**: Psychological shift to fall, indoor activities, winter preparation
- **Rosh Hashanah/Yom Kippur**: Cultural moment for Jewish consumers
- **Fall Auto Shows**: Industry shows and vehicle reveals (Detroit, Paris, etc.)

**Campaign Focus**: New model year launches, auto show coverage, back-to-school vehicles, fall buying season start

## Q4 (October - December)

### October
- **Fall Buying Season**: Peak buying season begins with optimal weather
- **Halloween**: Themed marketing, family vehicles for trick-or-treating
- **Diwali** (Hindu): Festival vehicles, celebration driving, family experiences
- **Fall Auto Shows**: Major international auto shows (Paris, Tokyo in odd years)
- **Fall Foliage Season**: Convertibles, scenic drive content, destination vehicles

**Campaign Focus**: Fall buying season, new model year push, scenic drive content, harvest season

### November
- **Thanksgiving** (US): Massive travel and family gathering vehicle demand
- **Black Friday/Cyber Monday**: Major sales events, manufacturer incentives peak
- **Movember**: Men's health awareness, male-skewed vehicle positioning
- **Holiday Gift Vehicles**: Luxury gift vehicles, high-end trim packages
- **Year-End Incentive Push**: Dealers and manufacturers push volume to hit annual targets

**Campaign Focus**: Thanksgiving travel vehicles, Black Friday deals, holiday gift vehicles, year-end incentives

### December
- **Holiday Season Peak**: Year-end vehicle purchases for tax benefits and gifts
- **Christmas/Hanukkah/Kwanzaa**: Holiday gift vehicles, luxury positioning
- **Year-End Inventory Clearance**: Aggressive discounting to clear all stock before year-end
- **Boxing Day** (UK/Canada): Secondary holiday sales event
- **New Year Financing**: 0% APR and favorable financing options common
- **New Year Resolutions**: Vehicle upgrade/purchase resolutions peak
- **Tax Planning**: Final purchases made for end-year tax implications

**Campaign Focus**: Year-end deals, holiday gift vehicles, financing promotions, January inventory clearance prep

## Cross-Seasonal Patterns

### Model Launch Windows
- **August Release**: New model year typically available late July-August
- **Spring Launches**: Mid-cycle refreshes and special editions April-June
- **Holiday Launches**: Limited editions and special vehicles November-December
- Use **cdp-data-enricher** to layer new model launches into customer interest tracking

### Buying Season Intensity
- **Spring (April-June)**: 15-20% above average sales
- **Fall (September-October)**: 10-15% above average sales
- **December**: 20-25% above average (year-end rush)
- **July-August**: 10% above average (summer travel, new model year)
- **January-March**: Below average (post-holiday spending fatigue)

### Test Drive Patterns
- **Optimal Test Drive Weather**: April-May, September-October
- **Weather Challenges**: Winter months (Dec-Feb) reduce test drive traffic 30-40%
- **Weekend Concentration**: 60-70% of test drives occur Saturdays and Sundays
- Plan campaigns 4-6 weeks before intended test drive date to build interest

### Financing & Incentive Cycles
- **Manufacturer Incentive Peaks**: August (new model year), November (holiday), December (year-end)
- **Dealer Incentive Peaks**: End of month, end of quarter, year-end
- **Financing Rates**: Best rates typically available during incentive periods
- **Trade-In Values**: Peak in spring/fall, lowest in winter

### Competitive Intensity
- **Black Friday/Cyber Monday**: Highest competitive promotional intensity
- **End of Month/Quarter**: Dealers and manufacturers most aggressive
- **New Model Launch**: Competing models launch similar timing (Toyota, Honda, Ford coordination)
- Differentiate through content and personalization, not just price
