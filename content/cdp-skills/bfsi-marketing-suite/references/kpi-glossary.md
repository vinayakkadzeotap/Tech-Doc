# BFSI KPI Glossary

Comprehensive reference for banking, financial services, insurance, and fintech key performance indicators with definitions, formulas, industry benchmarks, and customer analysis guidance.

## Customer Value Metrics

### Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV)

**Definition:** Total net profit expected from a customer over their entire relationship with the financial institution.

**Formula:**
```
CLTV = (Monthly Net Profit × Expected Relationship Months) - CAC
CLTV = Σ(Monthly Revenue - Monthly Cost) / (1 + Discount Rate)^month
```

**Example:**
- Monthly net profit: $15
- Expected relationship: 20 years (240 months)
- CAC: $200
- CLTV = ($15 × 240) - $200 = $3,400

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Retail banking: $1,500-5,000 (lifespan 20-30 years)
- Credit card customers: $500-2,000 (shorter lifespan)
- Mortgage customers: $5,000-20,000 (highest CLTV)
- Insurance customers: $2,000-8,000 (varies by product)
- Wealth management: $20,000-500,000+ (UHNWs)

**By Wealth Tier:**
- Mass market: $500-2,000
- Affluent: $5,000-20,000
- HNI: $50,000-500,000
- UHNW: $500,000-5,000,000+

**CLTV Drivers:**
- Products per customer (higher = higher CLTV)
- Longevity (tenure)
- Profitability (spread/margin)
- Cross-sell rate
- Churn prevention

**When it matters:** Customer acquisition budget, segment prioritization, retention ROI, profitability forecasting

---

### Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)

**Definition:** Total revenue divided by customer base (includes fees, interest, commissions).

**Formula:**
```
ARPU = (Interest Income + Fees + Commissions) / Total Customers
```

**Example:**
- Interest income: $10M
- Fee income: $5M
- Total customers: 1M
- ARPU = ($10M + $5M) / 1M = $15/month

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Retail banking: $10-30/month
- Wealth management: $50-500/month
- Insurance: $20-100/month
- Credit cards: $15-50/month

**By Product:**
- Savings account: $2-5/month
- Checking account: $5-15/month
- Mortgage: $50-200/month
- Credit card: $15-50/month
- Investment account: $20-100/month

**When it matters:** Pricing strategy, profitability, product mix optimization

---

### Average Assets Under Management (AUM)

**Definition:** Mean value of assets managed by wealth/investment division per customer.

**Formula:**
```
AUM = Total Assets Under Management / Number of Wealth Customers
```

**Example:**
- Total AUM: $1B
- Wealth customers: 1,000
- AUM per customer = $1M

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Mass market wealth: $50k-200k
- Affluent: $200k-$1M
- HNI: $1M-$10M
- UHNW: $10M+

**Growth Drivers:**
- New customer acquisitions
- Organic growth (market appreciation)
- Product mix improvements (higher-yielding assets)
- Inflow of new capital

**When it matters:** Wealth management business sizing, market share assessment, product allocation

## Products & Cross-Sell Metrics

### Products Per Customer (PPC)

**Definition:** Average number of distinct financial products held per customer.

**Formula:**
```
PPC = Total Products Sold / Total Customers
```

**Example:**
- Customers: 1M
- Accounts: Checking (1M), Savings (800k), Credit card (300k), Mortgage (150k), Investment (50k)
- Total products: 2.3M
- PPC = 2.3M / 1M = 2.3 products per customer

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Emerging: 1.2-1.5 products/customer (single product focus)
- Mature: 2.0-2.5 products/customer (multi-product adoption)
- Leader: 3.0-4.0+ products/customer (integrated ecosystem)

**Product Affinity (Typical Sequences):**
1. **Entry:** Savings account
2. **First cross-sell:** Checking account (if not primary)
3. **Second cross-sell:** Debit card
4. **Third cross-sell:** Credit card (income verification)
5. **Lending:** Personal loan or mortgage
6. **Investment:** Mutual funds, stocks
7. **Insurance:** Life, health, property
8. **Wealth:** Advanced investment, advisory services

**PPC by Wealth Tier:**
- Mass market: 1.5-2.0 products
- Affluent: 2.5-3.5 products
- HNI: 4.0-6.0 products
- UHNW: 6.0-10+ products

**Cross-Sell Growth Strategies:**
- Increase offer frequency
- Improve product recommendations (data analytics)
- Life event triggers (marriage, home purchase)
- Seasonal campaigns
- Digital activation (app highlights)

**When it matters:** Revenue growth, customer stickiness, wallet share, churn prevention

---

### Cross-Sell Rate

**Definition:** Percentage of customers purchasing an additional product within a period.

**Formula:**
```
Cross-Sell Rate % = Customers with new product / Eligible customers × 100
```

**Example:**
- Deposit customers: 100,000
- Purchased credit card: 8,000
- Cross-sell rate = 8,000 / 100,000 = 8%

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Retail banking: 5-15% annual cross-sell rate
- Wealth management: 10-25%
- Insurance: 8-20%
- Digital-first: 2-5% (lower due to single-product bias)

**By Product Pairing:**
- Deposit to credit card: 5-12% (easiest cross-sell)
- Deposit to lending: 3-8% (requires credit check)
- Lending to insurance: 10-20% (complementary, high value)
- Wealth to insurance: 15-30% (HNI focus area)

**Optimal Timing:**
- Deposit customer → 3-6 months stable account → credit card offer
- Mortgage customer → 6-12 months → home insurance offer
- Savings account → 12+ months → investment account offer

**When it matters:** Revenue growth, customer lifetime value, wallet share

## Customer Segmentation & Risk Metrics

### Net Promoter Score (NPS)

**Definition:** Measure of customer satisfaction and likelihood to recommend (-100 to +100).

**Formula:**
```
NPS = % Promoters (9-10) - % Detractors (0-6)
Range: -100 to +100
```

**Example:**
- 60% promoters (score 9-10)
- 10% detractors (score 0-6)
- NPS = 60% - 10% = +50

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Excellent: +70 or higher
- Good: +50 to +70
- Average: +30 to +50
- Poor: Below +30

**BFSI-Specific Insights:**
- Banking: Low NPS (avg +30-40, poor service reputation)
- Insurance: Very low NPS (avg +10-25, claims disputes)
- Fintech: Higher NPS (avg +40-60, better UX)
- Wealth management: High NPS (avg +50-70, personalized service)

**NPS Correlation with Churn:**
- Promoters: Low churn (<5% annual)
- Passives: Medium churn (10-20%)
- Detractors: High churn (40-60%)

**NPS by Segment:**
- VIP/HNI: Typically 20-30 points higher (white-glove service)
- Digital-first: Lower NPS (self-service frustration)
- Elderly: Higher NPS (relationship banking value)

**When it matters:** Service quality assessment, churn prediction, competitive positioning

---

### Non-Performing Assets (NPA) Ratio

**Definition:** Percentage of loan portfolio in default (payments overdue 90+ days).

**Formula:**
```
NPA Ratio % = Non-Performing Assets / Total Advances × 100
```

**Example:**
- Total loans outstanding: $1B
- Default loans (90+ days): $50M
- NPA ratio = 5.0%

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Strong banks: <2% NPA
- Average banks: 2-5% NPA
- Troubled banks: >5% NPA

**NPA by Loan Type:**
- Mortgage: 0.5-2.0% (secured, lower risk)
- Auto: 1-3% (secured, moderate risk)
- Personal: 3-8% (unsecured, higher risk)
- Credit card: 3-5% (unsecured, write-off risk)
- SME/business: 2-6% (economic cycle dependent)

**Trend Analysis:**
- Rising NPA: Economic stress, lending quality decline, early warning signal
- Stable NPA: Normal portfolio performance
- Declining NPA: Recovery improving, portfolio quality improving

**NPA Management Strategies:**
- Stricter underwriting (reduce new NPAs)
- Proactive collections (early intervention at 60 days)
- Restructuring programs (help customers avoid default)
- Write-offs (remove unsalvageable assets)

**When it matters:** Portfolio health, profitability impact, regulatory capital requirements, risk management

---

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)

**Definition:** Average cost to acquire one new customer.

**Formula:**
```
CAC = Total Acquisition Spending / New Customers Acquired
(Includes: Marketing, sales, promotion, incentives, operations)
```

**Example:**
- Total acquisition spend: $10M
- New customers: 50,000
- CAC = $10M / 50,000 = $200 per customer

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Retail banking: $100-500
- Digital banking: $20-100 (lower customer service cost)
- Credit cards: $50-300
- Wealth management: $500-5,000+
- Insurance: $100-400

**CAC Payback Period:**
```
Payback = CAC / Monthly Profit per Customer
Example: $200 CAC / $15 monthly profit = 13.3 months payback
```

**CAC by Channel:**
- Branch (in-person): $300-800 (high touch, expensive)
- Digital/online: $20-100 (self-service, efficient)
- Referral: $50-150 (cost-effective, quality customers)
- Partnership: $100-300 (co-marketing splits cost)
- Direct mail: $200-500 (medium efficiency)

**When it matters:** Acquisition strategy, channel mix optimization, profitability analysis

## Lending & Credit Metrics

### Loan-to-Value (LTV) Ratio

**Definition:** Loan amount as percentage of collateral/property value.

**Formula:**
```
LTV % = Loan Amount / Collateral Value × 100
```

**Example:**
- Home value: $500k
- Mortgage: $400k
- LTV = ($400k / $500k) × 100 = 80%

**Industry Standards:**
- Mortgage: 70-90% (lower = safer)
- Auto: 80-100% (can exceed 100% with trade-in)
- Personal: N/A (unsecured)

**Risk Implications:**
- <70% LTV: Low risk (strong equity cushion)
- 70-85% LTV: Moderate risk (standard conforming)
- 85-95% LTV: Higher risk (limited equity)
- >100% LTV: Very high risk (negative equity)

**Pricing Impact:**
- Lower LTV: Better rates (lower risk)
- Higher LTV: Higher rates (premium for risk)

**When it matters:** Mortgage origination, risk assessment, pricing strategy

---

### Debt Service Ratio (DSR) / Debt-to-Income Ratio

**Definition:** Monthly debt payments as percentage of gross monthly income.

**Formula:**
```
DSR % = (Proposed loan payment + Existing debt payments) / Gross Monthly Income × 100
```

**Example:**
- Proposed mortgage: $2,000/month
- Existing debt (auto, card): $500/month
- Gross income: $8,000/month
- DSR = ($2,000 + $500) / $8,000 × 100 = 31.25%

**Lending Standards:**
- Acceptable: <36% (healthy debt load)
- Caution: 36-43% (manageable but stressed)
- High risk: >43% (affordability concern)

**DSR Limits by Loan Type:**
- Mortgage: 28-36% primary, 43% total
- Auto: 10-15% of monthly income
- Personal: 5-10% of monthly income
- Credit card: <10% ideal

**Impact on Credit Score:**
- Ratio impacts credit utilization (part of score calculation)
- Higher DSR = lower credit score
- Lower DSR = higher credit score, better rates

**When it matters:** Loan underwriting, affordability assessment, default risk prediction

## Operational & Efficiency Metrics

### Cost-to-Income Ratio (CIR)

**Definition:** Operating costs as percentage of net revenue; efficiency measure.

**Formula:**
```
CIR % = Total Operating Costs / Net Revenue × 100
```

**Example:**
- Operating costs: $1B
- Net revenue: $2B
- CIR = 50% (50 cents cost per dollar revenue)

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- World-class banks: <40% (very efficient)
- Good banks: 40-50%
- Average: 50-60%
- Struggling: >60% (inefficient, unprofitable)

**CIR Components:**
- Personnel costs: 40-50% of total costs
- Technology/IT: 15-25%
- Infrastructure: 10-15%
- Other: 10-20%

**Digital Impact on CIR:**
- Branch-centric: High CIR (60%+)
- Digital-first: Low CIR (35-45%)
- Hybrid: Medium CIR (45-55%)

**Improvement Strategies:**
- Automation (reduce staff)
- Digital adoption (reduce branch costs)
- Revenue growth (fixed costs spread over more revenue)

**When it matters:** Profitability, operational efficiency, competitive positioning

## Profitability Metrics

### Net Interest Income (NII)

**Definition:** Interest earned on assets minus interest paid on deposits and borrowings.

**Formula:**
```
NII = Interest Income - Interest Expense
NII Margin % = (Interest Income - Interest Expense) / Average Assets × 100
```

**Example:**
- Interest income (loans): $500M
- Interest expense (deposits, funding): $300M
- NII = $200M

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- NII Margin: 2-4% of assets (varies by market)
- Developed markets: 2-3% (competitive, lower margins)
- Emerging markets: 3-5% (less competitive, higher margins)

**Margin Drivers:**
- Interest rate environment (higher rates = wider margins)
- Loan/deposit mix (mortgages have lower margins)
- Credit quality (defaults reduce actual margin)
- Funding costs (deposit rates impact expense side)

**When it matters:** Core profitability, lending rate strategy, competitive pricing

---

### Fee & Commission Income

**Definition:** Non-interest revenue from services (account fees, advisory, trading, insurance).

**Formula:**
```
Fee Income = Account fees + Advisory fees + Trading commissions + Service charges
```

**Trend:** Growing importance (move away from interest-dependent)

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Retail banking: 20-35% of total revenue (lower)
- Wealth management: 60-80% of revenue (fees on AUM)
- Investment banking: 85-95% of revenue (commission-based)
- Insurance: 100% of revenue (premium-based)

**When it matters:** Revenue diversification, product mix strategy, market cycle resilience

## Churn & Retention Metrics

### Customer Churn Rate

**Definition:** Percentage of customers discontinuing relationship in a period.

**Formula:**
```
Monthly Churn % = (Customers lost / Starting customers) × 100
```

**Example:**
- Starting: 1M customers
- Lost: 20,000
- Churn = 2% monthly (24% annualized)

**Industry Benchmarks:**

| Segment | Monthly | Annual |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Mortgage | 0.5% | 6% |
| Checking/Savings | 1-2% | 12-24% |
| Credit card | 2-4% | 24-48% |
| Insurance | 1-3% | 12-36% |
| Wealth management | 0.5-1.5% | 6-18% |
| Digital-first | 3-5% | 36-60% |

**Churn Reasons (typical):**
- Service quality/support: 30-40%
- Switching to competitor: 20-30%
- Price/rate changes: 15-25%
- Life event (relocation, retirement): 5-10%
- Account consolidation: 5-10%

**Churn Prediction Signals:**
- Declining balance (withdrawal trend)
- Reduced transaction frequency
- Missed loan payment
- Complaint history
- Delinquency rate increase

**How to Reduce:**
- Improve customer service
- Competitive pricing/rates
- Relationship strengthening (advisor touch)
- Proactive win-back (before churn)
- Product stickiness (multi-product customers churn less)

**When it matters:** Customer retention strategy, revenue stability, growth planning

---

## Digital Adoption Metrics

### Digital Adoption Rate

**Definition:** Percentage of customers using digital banking channels (mobile, online).

**Formula:**
```
Digital Adoption % = Active digital users / Total customers × 100
```

**Example:**
- Total customers: 5M
- Active digital users: 3M
- Adoption = 60%

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Mature markets: 60-80%
- Emerging markets: 30-50%
- Digital-first: 80-95%

**Digital Channel Usage:**
- Mobile banking: 40-70% (fastest growing)
- Online banking: 50-80%
- ATM: 60-85% (declining)
- Branch: 20-40% (declining for transactions)

**Impact on Business:**
- Cost reduction (digital cheaper than branch)
- Customer satisfaction (convenience)
- Data collection (behavioral insights)
- Engagement increase (easier, more frequent use)

**When it matters:** Digital transformation ROI, channel strategy, cost reduction targets
