## The 30-Second Elevator Pitch

<Callout type="tip" title="Memorize This">
"Zeotap is the enterprise CDP built for privacy-first markets. We help brands unify customer data from every touchpoint into a single profile, then activate it across 100+ destinations — all with GDPR baked into the architecture, not bolted on. Our customers typically see 30-40% improvement in marketing ROI within the first six months because they finally know who their customers actually are."
</Callout>

### Three Variations by Context

**At a conference (casual):**
> "You know how every brand says they want to be customer-centric, but their data is scattered across 20 different tools? We fix that. Zeotap unifies all that data into one profile per person, and we do it in a way that keeps your DPO happy. Think of us as the single source of truth for your customer data."

**In an executive briefing (strategic):**
> "Zeotap enables enterprises to operationalize their first-party data strategy. We provide deterministic identity resolution across channels, AI-powered segmentation, and real-time activation — built on a GDPR-first architecture that turns privacy compliance from a cost center into a competitive advantage."

**In a technical evaluation (precise):**
> "Zeotap ingests event-level and batch data from any source, resolves identities using our proprietary UCID graph with deterministic and probabilistic matching, supports real-time segment computation, and pushes unified profiles to 100+ pre-built destinations. Processing at 2M+ events per second, deployed on EU and US infrastructure."

## Value Propositions by Persona

Every deal has multiple stakeholders. Here is how to tailor your message to each one.

### CMO — Chief Marketing Officer

<Callout type="info" title="What the CMO Cares About">
Revenue growth, campaign performance, customer lifetime value, personalization at scale, proving marketing ROI to the board.
</Callout>

**Opening line:**
> "Your team is probably running campaigns across 10+ channels, but without a unified view of each customer, you're wasting budget on duplicates and missing high-value segments entirely."

**Key talking points:**
- **Unified customer view** — Stop guessing, start knowing. Every interaction across web, app, email, store, and call center in one profile.
- **AI-powered segmentation (Ada)** — Ada finds high-value segments your team would never build manually. Lookalike modeling, predictive churn, next-best-action.
- **Real-time personalization** — React to customer behavior in milliseconds, not days. Cart abandonment, browse intent, lifecycle triggers.
- **Measurable ROI** — Built-in attribution and reporting so you can prove the impact to the CFO.

**Talk track:**
> "One of our retail customers was running email campaigns to their entire database — 5 million contacts. After deploying Zeotap, they discovered 40% were duplicates or inactive. By focusing on verified, unified profiles and using Ada to identify high-propensity segments, they increased email revenue by 35% while sending 60% fewer emails. That is the kind of efficiency we deliver."

### CDO — Chief Data Officer

<Callout type="info" title="What the CDO Cares About">
Data quality, governance, single source of truth, breaking down silos, enabling data-driven decision making across the org.
</Callout>

**Opening line:**
> "How confident are you that your customer data is accurate, deduplicated, and accessible to every team that needs it?"

**Key talking points:**
- **Identity resolution (UCID)** — Deterministic matching across identifiers. Not just cookie-based — email, phone, device ID, CRM ID, loyalty ID all stitched together.
- **Data quality engine** — Automated deduplication, validation, and enrichment at ingestion.
- **Governance built in** — Consent management, data lineage, access controls, and audit trails.
- **Universal data layer** — One platform that feeds marketing, analytics, data science, and customer service.

**Talk track:**
> "Most enterprises I talk to have between 3 and 7 different versions of their customer data across different systems. The CDO knows this is a problem but stitching it together with internal engineering resources takes 12-18 months and still breaks. Zeotap gives you a governed, always-current unified customer dataset in weeks, not years."

### CTO — Chief Technology Officer

<Callout type="info" title="What the CTO Cares About">
Architecture, scalability, security, integration complexity, build-vs-buy, engineering resource allocation.
</Callout>

**Opening line:**
> "How much engineering time is your team spending on data pipelines and identity stitching today?"

**Key talking points:**
- **API-first architecture** — RESTful APIs, SDKs for web/mobile/server, webhook support. Fits into existing tech stack.
- **Scale** — 2M+ events/second processing. Battle-tested with enterprise workloads.
- **100+ pre-built connectors** — No custom integration work for major platforms (Google, Meta, Salesforce, Adobe, etc.).
- **EU/US deployment** — Data residency options. Infrastructure on major cloud providers.
- **Build vs. buy** — We have 200+ engineers who have spent 7+ years building this. Your team can focus on your core product.

**Talk track:**
> "I get it — your engineering team believes they can build this. And technically, they probably can build a basic version. But identity resolution at scale, real-time segment computation, maintaining 100+ destination connectors, staying current with privacy regulations — that is a full product team. Our customers typically estimate they would need 15-20 engineers for 18+ months to replicate what Zeotap provides out of the box."

### DPO — Data Protection Officer

<Callout type="info" title="What the DPO Cares About">
GDPR compliance, consent management, data subject rights, data processing agreements, vendor risk.
</Callout>

**Opening line:**
> "Unlike most CDPs that were built in the US and added GDPR compliance as an afterthought, Zeotap was born in Europe. Privacy is not a feature — it is our architecture."

**Key talking points:**
- **GDPR-first design** — Built in Berlin, compliance is foundational, not bolted on.
- **Consent orchestration** — Honors consent at the event level. If a user withdraws consent, it propagates to all downstream activations automatically.
- **Data subject rights** — Automated handling of access, deletion, and portability requests across the unified profile.
- **EU data residency** — Data stays in EU infrastructure. No transatlantic transfers required.
- **Certifications** — ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II, regular third-party audits.

**Talk track:**
> "One of the first questions a DPO asks is 'where does the data live?' With Zeotap, the answer is simple: in the EU, on infrastructure you can audit. We process consent at the event level — so when a customer opts out of email but keeps app notifications, that granularity is respected across every activation. We have passed privacy audits for some of the most regulated enterprises in Europe."

### The TCO Conversation

<Callout type="warning" title="When Price Comes Up Early">
If a prospect asks about pricing before understanding value, redirect: "I want to make sure we size this correctly for your needs. Can I first understand your current data stack and the use cases you want to solve? That way I can give you a proposal that maps directly to your ROI, not just a price sheet."
</Callout>

When you do discuss pricing, always frame it as total cost of ownership:

**Zeotap TCO = Platform license + implementation support**

**In-house alternative TCO = Engineering team (15-20 FTEs) + infrastructure + maintenance + connector updates + privacy compliance + opportunity cost of 18+ months delay**

> "When our customers do the math, they typically find that Zeotap costs 15-20x less than building and maintaining an equivalent solution in-house — and that is before you factor in the 12-18 months of lost revenue from delayed time-to-market."
