## Opening the Discovery Call

<Callout type="tip" title="The First 3 Minutes Matter Most">
Do not start by presenting Zeotap. Start by demonstrating that you understand their world. The goal of discovery is to listen 70% and talk 30%.
</Callout>

**Opening script:**
> "Thanks for taking the time. Before I share anything about Zeotap, I want to make sure I understand your current situation so I can focus on what is most relevant to you. Can you walk me through how your team currently manages customer data — from collection to activation?"

**Follow-up framing:**
> "That is helpful context. Let me ask a few more specific questions so I can understand where the biggest opportunities are for you."

## Questions by Pain Point

### Pain Point 1: Fragmented Customer Data

<Callout type="info" title="Signal You're Hearing This Pain">
"We have data everywhere." / "We don't have a single view of the customer." / "Our teams are working with different numbers."
</Callout>

**Discovery questions:**

1. "How many different systems hold customer data today?" *(Establishes scope — typical answer is 5-15)*
2. "When your marketing team wants to build a campaign audience, how do they get the data? How long does that process take?"
3. "If I asked your email team and your paid media team how many active customers you have, would they give me the same number?"
4. "What happens when a customer interacts across channels — say they browse on web, get an email, then call support? Can your team see that full journey?"
5. "Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?"

**What to listen for:**
- Number of data silos (more = bigger pain)
- Manual processes and workarounds (indicates urgency)
- Previous failed attempts (validates need, but watch for vendor fatigue)
- Specific examples of business impact (use these in your proposal)

### Pain Point 2: Identity Resolution Challenges

<Callout type="info" title="Signal You're Hearing This Pain">
"We can't match users across devices." / "Our match rates are terrible." / "We're losing track of customers when cookies go away."
</Callout>

**Discovery questions:**

1. "What identifiers do you use to recognize customers? Email, phone, device ID, loyalty ID?"
2. "What is your current match rate between online and offline customer data?"
3. "How are you preparing for the cookieless future? What is your first-party data strategy?"
4. "Do you have a concept of a unified customer ID today? How is it generated?"
5. "When a customer creates an account on web and then uses the mobile app, how long does it take for those profiles to merge?"

**What to listen for:**
- Low match rates (below 50% is painful)
- Dependence on third-party cookies (urgency driver)
- No existing identity graph (greenfield opportunity)
- Real-time vs. batch requirements

### Pain Point 3: Privacy and Compliance Pressure

<Callout type="info" title="Signal You're Hearing This Pain">
"Our legal team is blocking projects." / "We got flagged in an audit." / "We need to handle GDPR better."
</Callout>

**Discovery questions:**

1. "How do you currently manage consent across your digital properties?"
2. "If a customer submits a data deletion request, how long does it take to process across all your systems?"
3. "Has your DPO raised concerns about your current martech stack?"
4. "Are you operating across multiple jurisdictions? How do you handle different privacy regulations?"
5. "Have you had any compliance incidents or near-misses that are driving urgency here?"

**What to listen for:**
- Active DPO involvement (they will be a stakeholder — could be champion or blocker)
- Cross-border data requirements (Zeotap strength)
- Compliance deadlines (creates urgency)
- Previous incidents (strong motivator)

### Pain Point 4: Slow Time to Activation

<Callout type="info" title="Signal You're Hearing This Pain">
"It takes weeks to launch a campaign." / "We're always waiting on engineering." / "By the time we get the data, it's stale."
</Callout>

**Discovery questions:**

1. "Walk me through the process from when marketing defines a segment to when that audience is live in your ad platform. How many steps? How many teams involved?"
2. "How much engineering time goes into building and maintaining data pipelines for marketing?"
3. "How often does marketing request a new integration or connector? How long does it typically take?"
4. "Are you doing any real-time personalization today, or is everything batch-based?"
5. "If you could reduce activation time from weeks to hours, what would that mean for your business?"

**What to listen for:**
- Long cycle times (weeks/months indicates strong need)
- Engineering bottleneck (Zeotap removes this dependency)
- Requests for specific connectors (check if we support them)
- Real-time requirements (our differentiator)

### Pain Point 5: Proving Marketing ROI

<Callout type="info" title="Signal You're Hearing This Pain">
"We can't prove what's working." / "The board wants to see marketing ROI." / "We're cutting budget because we can't show results."
</Callout>

**Discovery questions:**

1. "How do you currently measure the ROI of your marketing campaigns?"
2. "Can you connect a specific campaign to revenue impact today?"
3. "What does your attribution model look like? Single-touch, multi-touch, or no formal model?"
4. "Is there pressure from leadership to justify marketing spend? What is that conversation like?"
5. "If you could prove that a specific segment generated 3x ROI, how would that change your budget conversations?"

**What to listen for:**
- No attribution model (opportunity for Zeotap insights)
- Budget pressure (creates urgency and executive sponsorship)
- Specific revenue targets (anchor your ROI case to these)

## Champion Identification Questions

<Callout type="important" title="No Champion = No Deal">
Every won deal has an internal champion. If you cannot identify one, the deal will stall. Ask these questions to find and develop your champion.
</Callout>

**Questions to identify a champion:**

1. "Who on your team feels the pain of fragmented data most acutely?"
2. "If this project were approved, who would own the implementation?"
3. "Who has been advocating for a CDP internally?"
4. "Is there someone on your team who has used a CDP before — maybe at a previous company?"
5. "Who would present the business case to leadership?"

**Questions to test champion strength:**

1. "Have you presented this initiative to your leadership yet? How did they respond?"
2. "If I gave you a business case document, would you be comfortable presenting it to the economic buyer?"
3. "What would need to be true for you to recommend Zeotap to your leadership?"
4. "Are there other stakeholders I should meet with? Can you facilitate those introductions?"

## Budget and Timeline Questions

<Expandable title="Budget Qualification" defaultOpen={true}>

**Direct approach (when you have rapport):**
> "Do you have budget allocated for a customer data platform this year?"

**Indirect approach (early stage):**
> "When you think about the investment required to solve this problem — not just a platform, but the people and process changes too — has your team started to scope that out?"

**Anchoring approach:**
> "For an enterprise deployment, our customers typically invest between X and Y annually. Does that align with what you had in mind, or should we be thinking about a phased approach?"

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Timeline Qualification" defaultOpen={false}>

**Key questions:**
1. "Is there a specific date or event driving the timeline for this decision?"
2. "When does your current contract expire?" *(If they have a competing tool)*
3. "If we started today, when would you need to see first results?"
4. "What does your procurement process look like? How long does vendor approval typically take?"
5. "Are there any board meetings or budget cycles that would affect timing?"

**Urgency boosters to listen for:**
- Contract renewal within 6 months
- Board mandate with a deadline
- Regulatory compliance deadline
- Upcoming product launch that needs personalization
- New fiscal year budget approval

</Expandable>

## After Discovery: Next Steps

<Callout type="success" title="Always End with Clear Next Steps">
Never end a discovery call without agreeing on the next step. Vague follow-ups kill deals.
</Callout>

**Closing the discovery call:**

> "Based on what you've shared, I think there is a strong fit here — particularly around [specific pain point they mentioned]. Here is what I'd recommend as a next step: I will put together a tailored overview that maps Zeotap's capabilities to the three use cases you mentioned — [use case 1], [use case 2], and [use case 3]. Then I would love to show you a focused demo with data that looks like yours. Can we get 45 minutes on [specific date] and include [champion name] and [other stakeholder they mentioned]?"

**What to document after every discovery call:**

| Field | What to Capture |
|-------|----------------|
| Pain points | Top 3, ranked by urgency |
| Key stakeholders | Names, titles, roles in decision |
| Current stack | Tools they use today, contracts, gaps |
| Budget status | Allocated, planned, or unbudgeted |
| Timeline | Decision date, implementation target |
| Competition | Other vendors in evaluation |
| Next step | Specific action with date and attendees |
| Champion | Who and how strong |
