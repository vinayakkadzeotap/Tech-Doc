## ⚔️ Competitive Battle Cards

Know your competition better than they know themselves. This module provides head-to-head battle cards for the five competitors you will encounter most often, with honest assessments of their strengths, exploitable weaknesses, and tactical traps to set during evaluations.

<KeyConcepts concepts={["Competitive Positioning", "Trap Questions", "Win Themes", "Weakness Exploitation", "Evaluation Criteria"]} />

<Callout type="warning" title="Rules of Competitive Engagement">
1. Never trash a competitor — it makes you look insecure.
2. Acknowledge their strengths honestly — it builds your credibility.
3. Steer the conversation to evaluation criteria where Zeotap wins.
4. Let the prospect discover the competitor's gaps through their own questions.
5. Always sell Zeotap's value, not against a competitor's weaknesses.
</Callout>

## Battle Card 1: Zeotap vs. Segment (Twilio)

<Callout type="info" title="When You'll Face Segment">
Segment is the most common competitor, especially when the prospect's engineering team is involved in the decision. Segment has strong brand recognition and a large developer community.
</Callout>

### Segment's Strengths (Acknowledge These)
- Strong developer experience and documentation
- Large ecosystem of integrations (300+ connectors)
- Good event tracking and data routing capabilities
- Well-known brand, especially in the US tech ecosystem
- Twilio backing provides stability and cross-sell opportunities

### Segment's Weaknesses (Exploit These)

<ComparisonTable
  headers={["Area", "Segment Limitation", "Zeotap Advantage"]}
  rows={[
    ["Identity resolution", "Basic identity stitching, relies heavily on known identifiers. No probabilistic matching.", "Proprietary UCID with deterministic + probabilistic matching. Higher match rates across channels."],
    ["Privacy/GDPR", "US-born architecture. GDPR compliance added as a layer, not foundational. Limited EU data residency options.", "European-born. GDPR is architectural, not a feature. Full EU data residency. Event-level consent tracking."],
    ["AI/ML capabilities", "Limited native AI. Primarily a data router — you need other tools for intelligence.", "Zoe AI engine built in: predictive segments, lookalike modeling, churn prediction, next-best-action."],
    ["Audience building", "Requires Personas add-on at significant extra cost. Limited segment computation.", "Full segment builder included. Real-time audience computation. No hidden add-on costs."],
    ["Enterprise support", "Tiered support model. Enterprise tier is expensive. Community forums for lower tiers.", "Dedicated CSM for every enterprise customer. Hands-on implementation support included."],
    ["Pricing model", "Event-based pricing can become very expensive at scale. Unpredictable costs.", "Predictable pricing based on profiles, not events. No surprise bills from traffic spikes."]
  ]}
/>

### Trap Questions to Suggest the Prospect Ask Segment

<Expandable title="Questions That Expose Segment's Gaps" defaultOpen={true}>

1. "Can you show us your probabilistic identity matching capabilities? What match rates do you achieve across anonymous and known users?"
2. "Where exactly will our EU customer data be processed and stored? Can you guarantee no transatlantic transfers?"
3. "What is included in the base platform vs. what requires Personas, Protocols, or other add-ons? Give us the all-in price."
4. "How does consent propagation work when a user opts out — does it automatically cascade to all connected destinations?"
5. "Show us your built-in predictive segmentation. Can you build a churn model or lookalike audience natively?"

</Expandable>

### How to Win Against Segment

> **Positioning statement:** "Segment is excellent at what it was built for — collecting events and routing them to your data warehouse. If that is all you need, it is a fine choice. But if you need a true CDP — identity resolution, unified profiles, intelligent segmentation, and privacy-first activation — you will find yourself buying add-ons and building custom solutions on top of Segment to get what Zeotap provides natively."

## Battle Card 2: Zeotap vs. mParticle

<Callout type="info" title="When You'll Face mParticle">
mParticle appears in deals where mobile app data is a key requirement, or where the prospect has a strong US-based digital product team.
</Callout>

### mParticle's Strengths (Acknowledge These)
- Strong mobile SDK and app data capabilities
- Good real-time data quality and transformation features
- Solid identity resolution for digital channels
- Developer-friendly API and documentation
- Good fit for digital-native companies

### mParticle's Weaknesses (Exploit These)

<ComparisonTable
  headers={["Area", "mParticle Limitation", "Zeotap Advantage"]}
  rows={[
    ["Enterprise scale", "Primarily serves mid-market digital companies. Limited enterprise reference customers in Europe.", "Battle-tested with large European enterprises across retail, finance, telco, and media."],

    ["European presence", "US-headquartered with limited European go-to-market and support.", "European-born with deep local market understanding, EU infrastructure, and local support teams."],

    ["Vertical expertise", "Horizontal platform without deep vertical solutions.", "Vertical-specific use cases and pre-built models for retail, finance, telco, and media."],

/>

### Trap Questions to Suggest the Prospect Ask mParticle

<Expandable title="Questions That Expose mParticle's Gaps" defaultOpen={false}>

1. "Can you show us enterprise reference customers in Europe with similar scale and use cases to ours?"
2. "How do you handle offline data integration — POS transactions, in-store interactions, call center records?"
3. "Walk us through your AI and predictive capabilities. Can you build a churn model or next-best-product recommendation natively?"

5. "Show us how consent propagation works for GDPR when a user exercises their right to erasure across all connected systems."

### How to Win Against mParticle

> **Positioning statement:** "mParticle is a strong choice for digital-native companies focused primarily on app and web data. If your data landscape extends beyond digital — into stores, call centers, CRM, and offline channels — and you operate in European markets with GDPR requirements, Zeotap provides the enterprise-grade, privacy-first platform that mParticle was not built for."

## Battle Card 3: Zeotap vs. Tealium

<Callout type="info" title="When You'll Face Tealium">
Tealium shows up in deals where the prospect has a strong tag management heritage or where IT/martech operations teams are leading the evaluation.

## Evaluation Criteria to Propose

<Callout type="success" title="Control the Evaluation">
Whenever possible, propose the evaluation criteria. The criteria you set determine who wins. Here are the criteria where Zeotap consistently leads.
</Callout>

When a prospect announces a formal evaluation, suggest they assess vendors on these criteria:

1. **Identity resolution quality** — Match rates across known and anonymous users, deterministic and probabilistic matching, cross-device stitching
2. **Privacy and compliance** — GDPR-native architecture, consent propagation, DSAR automation, data residency options, certifications
3. **AI and intelligence** — Predictive segmentation, automated audience discovery, lookalike modeling, churn prediction
4. **Time to value** — Implementation timeline to first activated use case, complexity of onboarding
5. **Total cost of ownership** — All-in pricing including add-ons, implementation services, ongoing support, and SI costs
6. **Ecosystem neutrality** — Equal activation quality across all major destinations, not just the vendor's own ecosystem
7. **Enterprise readiness** — Scale, reliability, support model, European references

These criteria play directly to Zeotap's strengths. If a competitor tries to shift the criteria to areas like developer community size, tag management, or ecosystem breadth, redirect:

> "Those are valid considerations, but the core question is: which platform best unifies your customer data, resolves identities, and activates intelligently while keeping you compliant? That is what a CDP evaluation should ultimately answer."
