## Retail: Omnichannel Personalization at Scale

### The Challenge

A major European fashion retailer with 500+ physical stores and a growing e-commerce presence was struggling with fragmented customer data. Their online and offline customer databases were completely separate — they had no way to know that the customer who browsed coats on the website was the same person who purchased shoes in-store last week.

**Specific pain points:**
- 8 separate data sources with no unified customer view
- Email campaigns sent to the entire database with no segmentation beyond basic demographics
- 40% of their loyalty program members could not be matched to online behavior
- Marketing team waited 2-3 weeks for engineering to build new audiences
- Estimated 25% of paid media budget wasted on duplicate targeting

<StatCard stats={[
  { value: "8", label: "Data Silos", color: "#ef4444" },
  { value: "40%", label: "Unmatched Loyalty Members", color: "#f59e0b" },
  { value: "2-3 wks", label: "Audience Build Time", color: "#ef4444" },
  { value: "25%", label: "Wasted Ad Spend", color: "#f59e0b" }
]} />

### The Solution

The retailer deployed Zeotap to unify their customer data across all channels and enable real-time personalization.

**Implementation highlights:**
- **Data unification** — Connected POS, e-commerce, mobile app, email, loyalty program, and customer service data into a single platform
- **Identity resolution (UCID)** — Matched online anonymous browsing to known loyalty profiles using deterministic and probabilistic matching
- **AI segmentation (Zoe)** — Deployed predictive models for purchase propensity, churn risk, and next-best-product recommendations
- **Real-time activation** — Connected to Google Ads, Meta, Criteo, Braze, and their in-store clienteling app

**Timeline:** 8 weeks from contract to first activated use case.

## Finance: Compliance-First Customer Intelligence

### The Challenge

A top-tier European bank needed to improve cross-sell effectiveness across its retail banking, insurance, and wealth management divisions. However, strict regulatory requirements and internal data governance policies made it nearly impossible to share customer data across business units.

**Specific pain points:**
- Three separate CRM systems across business units with no data sharing
- Compliance team blocked every data initiative due to consent uncertainty
- Cross-sell rate below 5% despite high customer overlap between divisions
- 6-month backlog of data requests from marketing to IT
- Failed a regulatory audit on data lineage documentation

<StatCard stats={[
  { value: "3", label: "Separate CRM Systems", color: "#ef4444" },
  { value: "<5%", label: "Cross-Sell Rate", color: "#f59e0b" },
  { value: "6 mo", label: "Data Request Backlog", color: "#ef4444" },
  { value: "Failed", label: "Regulatory Audit", color: "#ef4444" }
]} />

### The Solution

The bank selected Zeotap specifically for its GDPR-first architecture and granular consent management.

**Implementation highlights:**
- **Consent orchestration** — Implemented event-level consent tracking across all customer touchpoints, giving the DPO full visibility and control
- **Governed identity resolution** — Unified customer profiles across business units while respecting data access policies — each division only sees what consent allows
- **Compliant segmentation** — Built cross-sell segments that automatically exclude customers who have not consented to cross-divisional marketing
- **Audit trail** — Every data access, segment creation, and activation is logged with full lineage for regulatory reporting
- **EU data residency** — All data processed and stored within EU infrastructure

**Timeline:** 12 weeks including compliance review and DPO sign-off.

## Telco: Reducing Churn with Predictive Intelligence

### The Challenge

A major European telecommunications provider with 15 million subscribers was facing increasing churn in its prepaid segment. They had massive amounts of customer data — call records, data usage, recharge patterns, app usage, store visits — but it was siloed across network systems, billing platforms, and retail channels.

**Specific pain points:**
- 15% annual churn rate in prepaid segment (industry average: 10-12%)
- Churn prediction model based only on billing data, missing behavioral signals
- Retention offers sent to all at-risk customers regardless of value or likelihood to respond
- No ability to trigger real-time interventions when a customer showed churn behavior
- Customer acquisition cost 5x higher than retention cost, but budget skewed toward acquisition

<StatCard stats={[
  { value: "15%", label: "Annual Prepaid Churn", color: "#ef4444" },
  { value: "5x", label: "Acquisition vs Retention Cost", color: "#f59e0b" },
  { value: "15M", label: "Subscribers", color: "#3b82f6" },
  { value: "Low", label: "Retention Offer Precision", color: "#ef4444" }
]} />

### The Solution

The telco deployed Zeotap to create a unified subscriber profile and enable predictive churn management.

**Implementation highlights:**
- **Data unification** — Integrated network usage data, billing, CRM, app analytics, store interactions, and customer service records
- **Predictive churn modeling (Zoe)** — Built a multi-signal churn model that considers usage trends, recharge patterns, network experience (dropped calls, slow data), and competitive offer exposure
- **Micro-segmentation** — Created value-based churn segments: high-value-at-risk, winback-likely, and cost-to-serve-negative
- **Real-time triggers** — When a subscriber's usage pattern shifts toward churn indicators, a retention workflow triggers within minutes
- **Personalized retention offers** — Different offers based on subscriber value and predicted reason for churn

**Timeline:** 10 weeks to first activated churn model.

### The Results

<StatCard stats={[
  { value: "22%", label: "Churn Reduction (Year 1)", color: "#10b981" },

  { value: "Real-time", label: "Churn Intervention (from Monthly Batch)", color: "#3b82f6" }

**Quote to use:**
> "We went from sending generic retention offers to every at-risk subscriber to delivering personalized interventions at exactly the right moment. Zeotap's predictive engine catches churn signals we never knew existed in our data."

## Media: Audience Monetization and Subscriber Growth

### The Challenge

A leading European media group operating multiple digital news brands, streaming services, and podcast platforms needed to better understand their audiences to drive subscription growth and increase advertising revenue. Each brand operated independently with its own analytics and audience data.

**Specific pain points:**
- 5 separate brands with no cross-brand audience view
- Subscription conversion rate below 2% from free to paid
- Advertising sold on basic demographics, undervaluing premium audiences
- Content recommendation engine based only on within-session behavior
- No unified view of a reader across desktop, mobile, app, and newsletter

<StatCard stats={[
  { value: "5", label: "Brands with Separate Data", color: "#ef4444" },
  { value: "<2%", label: "Free-to-Paid Conversion", color: "#f59e0b" },
  { value: "Basic", label: "Ad Targeting Capability", color: "#ef4444" },
  { value: "Session", label: "Recommendation Context (Limited)", color: "#f59e0b" }
]} />

### The Solution

The media group deployed Zeotap as their unified audience platform across all brands.

**Implementation highlights:**
- **Cross-brand identity resolution** — Unified anonymous and known users across all 5 brands using UCID, discovering that 30% of users consumed content from multiple brands
- **Behavioral segmentation** — Built rich audience segments based on content consumption patterns, engagement depth, and topic affinity
- **Subscription propensity model (Zoe)** — Identified free users most likely to convert based on engagement patterns, visit frequency, and content consumption velocity
- **Premium audience packages** — Created first-party audience segments for advertisers (e.g., "Business Decision Makers," "Luxury Lifestyle," "Tech Enthusiasts") with verified reach numbers
- **Consent-based activation** — All audience activation respects GDPR consent, creating advertiser trust

**Timeline:** 8 weeks to first cross-brand audience insights.

## How to Tell a Case Study in 60 Seconds

<Callout type="success" title="The 60-Second Framework">

Use this structure when you need to reference a case study quickly in conversation:

1. **Context** (10 sec): "We work with a [vertical] company similar to yours..."
2. **Challenge** (15 sec): "They were struggling with [specific pain point the prospect shares]..."
3. **Action** (15 sec): "They deployed Zeotap to [specific solution]..."
4. **Result** (15 sec): "Within [timeframe], they saw [specific metric improvement]..."
5. **Bridge** (5 sec): "I think you could see similar results. Would you like me to walk through how?"

</Callout>
