# Value Engineering & ROI Tracking

Value engineering for a TAM is fundamentally different from sales-stage value selling. Sales quantifies the promise. TAMs quantify the proof. Your job is to instrument, measure, and communicate the actual value a customer is extracting from the Zeotap CDP — and to identify the gaps between what they are using and what they could be using. This is the single most important lever for renewals, expansions, and executive trust.

## Value Engineering Methodology for TAMs

Value engineering in the post-implementation phase follows a different rhythm than pre-sales. You are not projecting hypothetical gains — you are measuring real outcomes, attributing them to platform usage, and translating technical metrics into business language that stakeholders care about.

<Callout type="warning" title="Baseline or It Did Not Happen">
If you do not capture baseline metrics during onboarding, you cannot prove value later. The single biggest mistake TAMs make is skipping the baseline step because onboarding feels urgent. Block 30 minutes in your onboarding kickoff to document the customer's current-state metrics. Without a before, there is no measurable after.
</Callout>

### Dashboard Sections

<Expandable title="Section 1: Executive Summary" defaultOpen={true}>

The top of the dashboard should answer one question in 10 seconds: "Is the platform delivering value?"

Include these elements:
- **Overall ROI ratio**: Total quantified value divided by total platform cost (license + implementation + ongoing)
- **Value realized this period**: Sum of time savings, efficiency gains, revenue impact, and risk reduction in dollar terms
- **Cumulative value since go-live**: Running total to reinforce the long-term investment narrative
- **Health score**: A composite 0-100 score based on adoption, data quality, and activation breadth

Format the executive summary as a single row of four large stat cards. Executives scan, they do not read.
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Section 2: KPI Trends Over Time" defaultOpen={false}>

Line charts showing month-over-month trends for each technical KPI mapped to business outcomes. Always include the baseline as a reference line so the improvement is visually obvious.

Key charts to include:
- **Identity match rate trend** with baseline marker
- **Ingestion throughput trend** with SLA threshold line
- **Segment refresh latency trend** showing improvement over time
- **Activation success rate** with 99.5% target line
- **Profile completeness trend** with target threshold

Each chart should have a one-sentence annotation explaining what changed when there is a visible inflection point (e.g., "Match rate jumped 8% after adding email hashing in May").
</Expandable>

### Monthly Value Snapshot Template

Structure every monthly snapshot identically so customers can compare across months:

1. **Value Delivered This Month**: Top 3 quantified outcomes (e.g., "Activated 2.4M profiles to Meta with 94% match rate, up from 78% at baseline")
2. **KPI Dashboard**: 4-6 key metrics with trend arrows (up/down/stable vs last month)
3. **Issues Resolved**: Any data quality incidents, support tickets, or configuration changes completed
4. **Proactive Recommendations**: 1-2 actions the customer should take next month to increase value
5. **Looking Ahead**: Any platform updates, new features, or upcoming milestones relevant to this account

<Callout type="tip" title="The 48-Hour Rule for Monthly Reports">
Send the monthly value snapshot within the first 48 hours of the new month. Customers notice when reports are late, and late reports signal that their account is not a priority. Block time on the last day of each month to prepare the report so it is ready to send on the 1st or 2nd.
</Callout>

### QBR Value Presentation Structure

<Expandable title="Quarterly Business Review Deck Template" defaultOpen={true}>

**Slide 1: Executive Summary**
- One-sentence value statement: "In Q[X], Zeotap delivered $[X] in measurable value across revenue lift, cost savings, and operational efficiency."
- Three key metrics in large font with trend indicators

**Slide 2: Value Delivered This Quarter**
- Value log highlights organized by category: Revenue Impact, Cost Savings, Time Savings, Risk Reduction
- Dollar values where available, qualitative evidence where not

**Slide 3: Technical KPI Performance**
- All mapped KPIs with baseline reference, current value, and trend
- Annotations on any significant changes

**Slide 4: Platform Adoption and Utilization**
- Feature utilization table with expansion opportunities highlighted
- Active users trend and team adoption metrics

**Slide 5: ROI Summary**
- Running total of value delivered since engagement started
- ROI calculation: (Total Value Delivered - Platform Cost) / Platform Cost
- Trend showing ROI improving over time as adoption deepens

**Slide 6: Case Study Highlight**
- One detailed win story from this quarter with specific numbers
- Customer quote if available

**Slide 7: Roadmap and Next Quarter Plan**
- Upcoming platform features relevant to this customer
- Proposed new use cases with projected value
- Required actions from the customer's team

**Slide 8: Ask / Call to Action**
- Specific, concrete request: approve a new use case, expand to a new team, add a data source, renew
- Clear next steps with owners and dates
</Expandable>

### The Value Log: Your Most Important Document

Maintain a running document for each account that captures value evidence as it happens. Do not wait until QBR prep to remember what value was delivered.

| Field | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Date | When the value was realized | 2026-02-14 |
| Value Type | Technical, Business, or Strategic | Business |
| Description | What happened | Launched suppression audience that eliminated ad waste to 340K existing customers |
| Evidence | Data, screenshot, or customer quote | Platform export showing 340K profiles suppressed; customer confirmed $42K monthly savings |
| Dollar Impact | Estimated financial impact | $42,000/month = $504,000/year |
| Attribution | How much is attributable to Zeotap | 90% — suppression was not possible before Zeotap |

<Callout type="warning" title="The Attribution Trap">
Never claim 100% attribution for a business outcome unless you can prove it with an A/B test. Marketing outcomes are always multi-causal. A credible TAM says: "Zeotap audiences contributed to a 25% ROAS improvement alongside creative optimizations and seasonal trends." Overclaiming gets caught and destroys trust permanently. Shared credit builds more trust than sole credit ever could.
</Callout>

### Building the Incremental Value Narrative

When presenting value over time, use this five-part framework:

1. **Anchor to baseline**: Always start with where the customer was before Zeotap
2. **Show the trajectory**: Month-over-month improvements, not just current state
3. **Highlight inflection points**: Call out specific moments when value accelerated (new data source added, new use case launched, team training completed)
4. **Project forward**: Based on current trajectory, show what value will look like in 6-12 months if the customer continues to expand usage
5. **Quantify the gap**: Show the difference between current value and potential value if underutilized capabilities are activated

### Case Study Data Points to Capture

<Expandable title="Metrics That Make Case Studies Compelling" defaultOpen={true}>

Every case study needs hard numbers. Capture these from your ROI dashboard and customer conversations:

**Efficiency Metrics**:
- Time saved on audience creation (hours per week)
- Reduction in data onboarding time (weeks to days or hours)
- Decrease in manual data wrangling effort (FTE hours saved per month)
- Number of point solutions consolidated

**Performance Metrics**:
- Improvement in campaign match rates (percentage points gained)
- Increase in addressable audience size (absolute number or percentage)
- Improvement in segment precision (conversion rate lift from A/B tests)
- Reduction in wasted ad spend via suppression

**Business Outcome Metrics**:
- Revenue attributed to Zeotap-powered campaigns (with attribution methodology)
- Cost reduction from eliminated tools or manual processes (annual dollar savings)
- Time-to-market improvement for new campaigns or audiences
- Compliance risk reduction (quantified in faster DSR response time or audit readiness)

**Scale Metrics**:
- Total profiles managed in the platform
- Events processed per day
- Number of active destination connectors
- Number of team members using the platform regularly
- Number of active audience segments
</Expandable>

### Case Study Narrative Structure

Use this framework for every case study:

| Section | Content | Length |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Challenge | What was the customer struggling with before Zeotap? Use their words. | 2-3 sentences |
| Solution | What specific Zeotap capabilities addressed the challenge? Be technical but accessible. | 3-4 sentences |
| Implementation | How long did it take? What was the TAM's role? What was surprising? | 2-3 sentences |
| Results | Hard numbers: before vs after for each key metric. Use the value log. | 4-6 bullet points with numbers |
| Customer Quote | A direct quote from your champion about the impact. | 1-2 sentences |
| What is Next | What additional value is the customer planning to unlock? | 1-2 sentences |

<Callout type="info" title="The Best Time to Ask for a Case Study">
Ask for case study participation immediately after a successful QBR where the customer is visibly pleased with the results. Do not wait. The emotional high from a great QBR is the moment when customers are most likely to say yes. Prepare a one-paragraph email template you can send within 24 hours of the QBR.

### Risk Signal Reference Table

<Expandable title="Early Warning Indicators — Detect 3-6 Months Before Renewal" defaultOpen={true}>

| Risk Signal | Measurement | Yellow Threshold | Red Threshold | Recommended Action |
|------------|-------------|-----------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Login frequency declining | Unique users per week | 30% drop from 90-day average | 60% drop from 90-day average | Reach out to champion, offer training refresher |
| Audience creation rate dropping | New segments created per month | 40% decline over 2 months | No new segments in 30 days | Investigate if team had turnover, offer use case workshop |
| Ingestion volume flat or declining | Events per day trend | Flat for 60 days | Declining for 30 days | Check if data sources were disconnected, review data strategy |
| Support ticket sentiment | Tone and frequency of tickets | Increasing frustration in ticket language | Multiple escalations in 30 days | Proactive outreach, offer dedicated support sprint |
| Champion engagement declining | Response time to TAM communications | Response time doubles | No response for 2+ weeks | Multi-thread to other contacts, escalate internally |
| Feature utilization narrowing | Number of platform features used monthly | Usage concentrating to 1-2 features only | Only using basic segmentation | Build and present a value acceleration plan |
| QBR attendance declining | Seniority level of attendees | VP stops attending | Only junior analyst attending | Re-engage executive sponsor with a tailored value brief |
| Competitive evaluation signals | Customer asking about competitor features | Questions about specific competitor capabilities | Formal RFP or proof of concept with competitor | Engage product team for competitive response, accelerate value delivery |
</Expandable>

<Callout type="warning" title="The Silent Churn Pattern">
The most dangerous at-risk accounts are not the ones filing angry support tickets. They are the ones that go quiet. Declining logins, fewer questions, shorter email responses, skipped QBRs — these are the signals of a customer who has mentally moved on. If you see three or more yellow signals simultaneously, treat the account as red regardless of individual thresholds.
</Callout>

### Renewal Risk Response Playbook

<Expandable title="Actions by Risk Level" defaultOpen={false}>

**Low Risk (All Green)**:
- Continue standard value reporting cadence
- Focus on expansion opportunities
- Prepare renewal proposal 90 days before contract end
- Position renewal as a formality backed by data

**Medium Risk (Yellow Signals)**:
- Increase touchpoint frequency (weekly instead of bi-weekly)
- Schedule a dedicated value acceleration session with the customer's team
- Identify and address the root cause of declining engagement
- Prepare a 30-day plan to demonstrate quick wins
- Brief your account executive on the risk and your mitigation plan

**High Risk (Red Signals)**:
- Escalate to your TAM manager and account executive within 24 hours
- Request executive sponsor engagement (your VP to their VP)
- Launch a rapid value demonstration sprint: 2-week focused effort to deliver a visible win
- Prepare a competitive defense brief if competitor evaluation is suspected
- Document all value delivered to date for the save conversation
- Consider offering professional services or training at no cost to re-engage the team
</Expandable>

### Milestone Tracking Process

For each account, maintain a milestone tracker that records:

1. **Target date**: When you expect the customer to reach each milestone based on their implementation plan
2. **Actual date**: When they actually reached it
3. **Variance**: Days ahead or behind schedule
4. **Blockers**: What slowed them down (if delayed) — categorize as customer-side, platform-side, or external
5. **Accelerators**: What helped them move faster (if ahead of schedule)
6. **Value evidence**: Specific metrics that prove the milestone was achieved

<Expandable title="Milestone Review Cadence" defaultOpen={false}>

- **Weekly (internal)**: Review milestone progress for all accounts. Identify any accounts stalled between milestones for more than 2 weeks and investigate the blocker.
- **Bi-weekly (with customer)**: During regular syncs, reference the next milestone and discuss what is needed to reach it. This frames every conversation around forward progress rather than reactive problem-solving.
- **Quarterly (QBR)**: Present the full milestone map showing achieved milestones, current position, and the path forward. This visual narrative of structured progress is especially powerful for executive audiences.
- **At renewal**: Present the complete milestone journey from M1 through current state. A visual timeline showing consistent progress is more compelling than any single ROI number because it demonstrates the depth of the partnership.
</Expandable>

<Callout type="tip" title="The TAM Value Flywheel in Practice">
Measure value, then communicate value. When customers see proof, they trust you more. When they trust you more, they adopt more features. When they adopt more features, more value is created. Measure that value, communicate it, and the flywheel accelerates. This is what separates transactional support relationships from strategic partnerships. Once the flywheel is spinning, renewals and expansions become natural outcomes rather than stressful negotiations.

### Weekly Operating Rhythm for Value-Driven TAMs

| Day | Activity | Time Investment | Output |
|-----|---------|----------------|--------|
| Monday | Review ROI dashboards and health metrics for all accounts | 45 min | Flagged accounts with declining metrics or risk signals |
| Tuesday-Thursday | Customer calls and syncs — reference value data in every conversation | Ongoing | Value-informed conversations, not reactive status updates |
| Friday | Update milestone trackers, draft any monthly snapshots due next week | 30 min | Current milestone status, draft reports ready for review |
| Last day of month | Finalize and send monthly value snapshots for all accounts | 60 min | Monthly reports delivered within 48 hours of month end |
| End of quarter | Prepare and deliver QBR presentations | 2-3 hours per account | Comprehensive value review with expansion recommendations |

The TAMs who consistently execute this operating model are the ones whose accounts renew at the highest rates, expand the fastest, and generate the most customer advocacy. Value engineering is not an optional add-on to the TAM role — it is the core of the role. Build your value practice with discipline, maintain your value log with rigor, and present your findings with confidence and honesty. The data will do the persuading for you.
