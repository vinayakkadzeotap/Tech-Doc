# Value Engineering & ROI Tracking

Value engineering is the discipline of systematically measuring, documenting, and communicating the value a customer receives from Zeotap. Without it, renewals become price negotiations instead of strategic conversations. TAMs who master value engineering protect revenue, accelerate expansion, and build executive-level relationships that competitors cannot displace.

## The Value Framework

Value does not materialize at the moment of renewal. It builds over time across three layers. The TAM's job is to track value at each layer and connect them into a coherent story that resonates with both technical and executive stakeholders.

### Layer 1: Technical Value (Months 1-3)

Technical value is the foundation. It answers the question: "Is the platform working, and is the customer's team using it?"

<Expandable title="Technical Value Metrics" defaultOpen={true}>

| Metric | What It Measures | How to Capture |
|--------|------------------|----------------|
| Integration completeness | % of planned data sources connected and ingesting | Onboarding tracker |
| Identity match rate | % of events resolved to a unified profile | Platform dashboard |
| Platform adoption | Number of active users, login frequency, features used | Usage analytics |
| Data quality score | Composite of completeness, accuracy, freshness, consistency | Data quality dashboard |
| Time to first audience | Days from contract signing to first audience activated | Onboarding timeline |
| Destinations connected | Number of activation destinations configured and syncing | Platform configuration |

**How to communicate technical value**: Technical value resonates with the customer's data engineering and marketing operations teams. Present it in terms of operational outcomes: "Your identity graph is resolving 78% of web traffic to known profiles, up from 0% before Zeotap. Your team built and activated 15 audiences in the first 90 days without writing any code."
</Expandable>

### Layer 2: Business Value (Months 3-6)

Business value translates technical capabilities into measurable business outcomes. It answers the question: "Is the platform making the customer money or saving them money?"

<Expandable title="Business Value Metrics" defaultOpen={true}>

| Metric | What It Measures | How to Calculate |
|--------|------------------|------------------|
| ROAS improvement | Return on ad spend for campaigns using Zeotap audiences vs. control | (Revenue from Zeotap audiences / Ad spend) vs. (Revenue from control / Ad spend) |
| Customer acquisition cost reduction | Cost per acquisition using Zeotap audiences vs. legacy targeting | (Total acquisition spend / New customers) before vs. after |
| Wasted ad spend eliminated | Dollar value of impressions saved by suppressing existing customers | Suppressed audience size x CPM x estimated impressions |
| Conversion rate lift | Improvement in conversion for Zeotap-powered campaigns | A/B test: Zeotap audience conversion rate vs. control |
| Time saved | Hours saved by marketing team using Zeotap vs. manual processes | Survey marketing team: hours per week before vs. after |
| Speed to activation | Time from segment idea to live campaign | Measure: segment creation to destination sync completion |
</Expandable>

### Layer 3: Strategic Value (Months 6-12)

Strategic value is the hardest to quantify but the most powerful at renewal. It answers the question: "Is Zeotap a commodity tool or a strategic platform that enables the customer's data strategy?"

<Expandable title="Strategic Value Indicators" defaultOpen={false}>

| Indicator | What It Signals | How to Assess |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| Multi-team adoption | Platform is embedded across the organization, not siloed | Count distinct teams/departments using the platform |
| Executive sponsorship | C-level stakeholder actively advocates for the platform | Executive mentions Zeotap in internal communications or external presentations |
| Roadmap alignment | Customer's product roadmap depends on Zeotap capabilities | Customer requests specific features or integrations |
| Competitive displacement | Customer has consolidated from multiple point solutions to Zeotap | Number of tools replaced or deprecated |
| Data strategy enablement | Zeotap is the backbone of the customer's customer data strategy | Customer references Zeotap in their data strategy documents |
| Innovation partnership | Customer participates in beta programs, co-development, or case studies | Active engagement in product feedback loops |
</Expandable>

## How to Track and Quantify Value Delivered

Value tracking is an ongoing discipline, not a one-time exercise before renewal. Build value evidence throughout the engagement so that when renewal comes, the story tells itself.

### The Value Log

Maintain a running document for each account that captures value evidence as it happens. Do not wait until QBR prep to remember what value was delivered. The value log should contain:

- **Date**: When the value was realized
- **Value type**: Technical, Business, or Strategic
- **Description**: What happened (e.g., "Launched lookalike audience that outperformed legacy targeting by 35%")
- **Evidence**: Data, screenshot, or customer quote that supports the claim
- **Dollar impact**: Estimated financial impact (if applicable)
- **Attribution**: How much of this outcome is attributable to Zeotap (be honest; shared credit builds more trust than overclaiming)

<Callout type="warning" title="The Attribution Trap">
Never claim 100% attribution for a business outcome unless you can prove it with an A/B test. Marketing outcomes are always multi-causal. A credible TAM says: "Zeotap audiences contributed to a 25% ROAS improvement alongside creative optimizations and seasonal trends." An incredible TAM who overclaims gets caught and loses trust permanently.
</Callout>

## Building the Customer's ROI Story

The ROI story is a narrative that connects the investment in Zeotap to measurable returns. It should be compelling enough that your champion can retell it to their CFO without you in the room.

### ROI Calculation Framework

<Expandable title="Revenue Impact ROI" defaultOpen={true}>

**Formula**: ROI = (Incremental Revenue Attributable to Zeotap - Zeotap Annual Cost) / Zeotap Annual Cost x 100

**Example**:
- Zeotap annual cost: $200,000
- Incremental revenue from Zeotap-powered campaigns: $1,200,000 (measured via A/B test lift)
- Attribution factor: 40% (Zeotap was one of several contributing factors)
- Attributable incremental revenue: $480,000
- ROI: ($480,000 - $200,000) / $200,000 x 100 = **140% ROI**
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Cost Savings ROI" defaultOpen={false}>

**Formula**: ROI = (Annual Cost Savings from Zeotap) / Zeotap Annual Cost x 100

**Components of cost savings**:
- Wasted ad spend eliminated via suppression: Calculate suppressed audience size x CPM x impression frequency
- Tools consolidated or replaced: Sum of annual cost of displaced tools
- Team time saved: Hours saved per week x loaded hourly cost x 52 weeks
- Reduced agency dependency: Decrease in agency hours for audience management

**Example**:
- Zeotap annual cost: $200,000
- Suppression savings: $150,000/year
- Replaced tools (legacy DMP + manual segment builder): $120,000/year
- Team time saved (10 hrs/week x $75/hr): $39,000/year
- Total savings: $309,000/year
- ROI: ($309,000 - $200,000) / $200,000 x 100 = **54.5% ROI**
</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Combined ROI Story" defaultOpen={false}>

The most compelling ROI story combines revenue impact and cost savings:

| Value Category | Annual Impact |
|---|---|
| Incremental revenue (attributable) | $480,000 |
| Wasted ad spend eliminated | $150,000 |
| Tools consolidated | $120,000 |
| Team efficiency gains | $39,000 |
| **Total value delivered** | **$789,000** |
| **Zeotap annual cost** | **$200,000** |
| **Net value** | **$589,000** |
| **ROI** | **294.5%** |

This is the table your champion brings to the CFO. Make it easy for them.
</Expandable>

### QBR Value Presentation Template

<Expandable title="Executive Business Review Deck Structure" defaultOpen={true}>

**Slide 1: Executive Summary**
- One-sentence value statement: "In Q[X], Zeotap delivered $[X] in measurable value across revenue lift, cost savings, and operational efficiency."
- Three key metrics in large font

**Slide 2: Value Delivered This Quarter**
- Value log highlights organized by Technical, Business, Strategic
- Dollar values where available, qualitative evidence where not

**Slide 3: Platform Adoption & Health**
- Active users trend, feature adoption heatmap, data quality scorecard
- Comparison to previous quarter

**Slide 4: Campaign Performance**
- Top-performing audiences and their business outcomes
- A/B test results with lift percentages
- Suppression impact analysis

**Slide 5: ROI Summary**
- Running total of value delivered since engagement started
- ROI calculation (revenue impact + cost savings vs. investment)
- Trend showing ROI improving over time

**Slide 6: Roadmap & Next Quarter Plan**
- What is coming on the Zeotap product roadmap that benefits this customer
- Proposed objectives and use cases for next quarter
- Resources needed from the customer

**Slide 7: Ask / Call to Action**
- Specific request: expand to new team, add new data source, approve next use case, renew
- Clear next steps with owners and dates
</Expandable>

## Presenting Value at Executive Reviews

The way you present value matters as much as the value itself. Executives have limited attention and strong pattern recognition for vendor spin.

<Callout type="tip" title="The 3-Minute Rule">
If your executive sponsor leaves the room after 3 minutes, they should have heard the most important number, the biggest win, and the one thing you need from them. Front-load your presentation with impact. Save the details for the appendix.
</Callout>

### Do's and Don'ts of Executive Value Presentations

<ComparisonTable
  headers={["Do", "Don't"]}
  rows={[
    ["Lead with the customer's business outcomes", "Lead with your product features"],
    ["Use the customer's own language and KPIs", "Use Zeotap-specific jargon"],
    ["Show trends over time (improving trajectory)", "Show a single snapshot with no context"],
    ["Be honest about attribution (shared credit)", "Claim 100% credit for multi-factor outcomes"],
    ["Include customer quotes and testimonials", "Only show your own data and charts"],
    ["End with a specific, reasonable ask", "End with a generic 'any questions?'"],
    ["Prepare a 1-page executive summary", "Send a 40-page deck in advance"],
    ["Bring data that the customer cannot generate themselves", "Repeat data the customer already has"]
  ]}
  highlightColumn={0}
/>

<Callout type="important" title="Value Engineering Is Renewal Insurance">
Start building the value story in month one, not month eleven. By the time renewal conversations begin, you should have 9-12 months of documented value evidence that makes the renewal decision obvious. If you are scrambling to find value proof at renewal time, you have already lost the strategic position.
</Callout>

Value engineering transforms the TAM role from technical support to strategic partnership. When you can walk into a room and show a CFO that their $200K investment generated $789K in measurable returns, the conversation shifts from "Should we renew?" to "Where else can we apply this?" That is the goal. Build your value practice with discipline, maintain your value log with rigor, and present your findings with confidence and honesty.
