## 📊 What is a Customer Data Platform?

A **Customer Data Platform (CDP)** is software that creates a **persistent, unified customer database** that is accessible to other systems. Think of it as the "single source of truth" for everything you know about each customer.

<Callout type="tip" title="The Simple Explanation">
Imagine you could merge your CRM, website analytics, mobile app data, email data, and purchase history into **one complete profile** per customer — that's what a CDP does.
</Callout>

### Why Do Companies Need a CDP?

Before CDPs, companies faced this reality:

- **Marketing team** uses one set of tools (email platform, ad platforms)
- **Sales team** uses a CRM with different customer data
- **Support team** has ticket data in a helpdesk tool
- **Product team** has analytics from the app
- **Finance team** has transaction data in a billing system

**Nobody has the complete picture.** A customer might get a promotional email for a product they already bought, or a support agent might not know the customer's recent website activity.

A CDP solves this by:
1. **Ingesting data** from all these sources
2. **Resolving identity** — figuring out that `user123@gmail.com`, `device_xyz`, and `CRM_lead_456` are all the same person
3. **Building a unified profile** with all their data in one place
4. **Making it actionable** — pushing this unified view to every team's tools

<Callout type="important" title="DMPs Are Dying">
Data Management Platforms (DMPs) relied on third-party cookies, which browsers are deprecating. CDPs use first-party data — the data you collect directly from your customers — making them future-proof.
</Callout>

### The "Golden Record" Concept

The core idea behind a CDP is the **Golden Record** — the single, most accurate, most complete representation of each customer. When data conflicts exist (e.g., two different phone numbers), the CDP uses rules to determine which is the "golden" value.
