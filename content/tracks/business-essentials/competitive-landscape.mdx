### Head-to-Head Comparisons

<Expandable title="Zeotap vs. Segment (Twilio)" defaultOpen={true}>

**Segment** is the most well-known CDP, acquired by Twilio. It's developer-focused and strong at data routing.

| Dimension | Zeotap | Segment |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **Identity Resolution** | Best-in-class (deterministic + probabilistic) | Basic (relies on known IDs) |
| **Privacy/GDPR** | EU-born, privacy-first architecture | US-born, GDPR added later |
| **Audience Builder** | Visual + AI (Zoe) | Basic, requires SQL for complex segments |
| **Customer Journeys** | Built-in journey canvas | No — requires Twilio Engage (extra cost) |
| **Target Buyer** | Marketing + Data teams | Engineering teams |
| **Pricing** | Platform fee + usage | Per-event pricing (can get expensive) |

**When we win:** Enterprise deals where identity resolution, GDPR compliance, and marketing usability matter.

**When they win:** Developer-led organizations that want a lightweight data routing layer.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Zeotap vs. mParticle">

**mParticle** is a developer-focused CDP strong in mobile data.

| Dimension | Zeotap | mParticle |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **Identity Resolution** | Full graph (UCID) | Profile-level stitching |
| **Mobile Strength** | Good SDK coverage | Excellent mobile SDK |
| **AI/ML** | Zoe AI, propensity models | Limited built-in AI |
| **EU/Privacy** | EU-first | US-first |
| **Audience Builder** | Visual + NLP | API-driven |

**When we win:** European enterprises, complex identity challenges, marketing-led buyers.

**When they win:** US mobile-first companies, developer-led purchases.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Zeotap vs. Tealium">

**Tealium** is one of the oldest CDPs, evolved from tag management.

| Dimension | Zeotap | Tealium |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **Origin** | Purpose-built CDP | Evolved from tag management |
| **Identity** | Advanced graph-based | Good but less sophisticated |
| **Real-Time** | Millisecond processing | Good real-time capabilities |
| **AI** | Zoe AI assistant | ML Workbench (requires expertise) |
| **Market Position** | Growing challenger | Established incumbent |

**When we win:** AI-powered segmentation, modern architecture, better UX.

**When they win:** Existing Tealium tag management customers, established relationships.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Zeotap vs. Suite CDPs (Adobe, Salesforce)">

**Adobe CDP** and **Salesforce CDP** are part of larger marketing cloud suites.

| Dimension | Zeotap | Suite CDPs |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **Independence** | Best-of-breed, vendor neutral | Locked into suite ecosystem |
| **Implementation** | Weeks | Months (complex suite integration) |
| **Cost** | Competitive | Very expensive (suite pricing) |
| **Flexibility** | Works with any stack | Best with their own tools |
| **Identity** | Superior cross-device | Focused on known users |

**When we win:** Companies that want a best-of-breed CDP without vendor lock-in, faster time-to-value, and lower cost.

**When they win:** Companies already deeply invested in Adobe or Salesforce ecosystem.

</Expandable>

### Our Differentiators — Summary

<Callout type="important" title="Remember These Five">

1. **Identity Resolution** — Our UCID engine is best-in-class at unifying cross-device, cross-channel identities
2. **EU-First Privacy** — Built in Germany with GDPR at the architecture level, not bolted on
3. **AI-Powered (Zoe)** — Non-technical users can build segments using natural language
4. **Speed** — Real-time processing (milliseconds), not batch (hours)
5. **Enterprise Scale** — Billions of profiles, millions of events per second

</Callout>

### When We Lose — Honest Assessment

Understanding where we're weaker helps us improve:

- **Developer mindshare** — Segment has stronger brand recognition among developers
- **Mobile-first companies** — mParticle has deeper mobile SDK capabilities
- **Suite-locked enterprises** — Hard to displace when a customer is all-in on Adobe or Salesforce
- **Self-serve / PLG** — Our sales-led GTM doesn't serve the self-serve market well
- **US market presence** — Stronger in Europe than in the US currently

<Callout type="tip" title="Don't Hide Weaknesses">
In sales conversations, acknowledging where competitors are strong builds credibility. Prospects know no product is perfect. Focus on the dimensions that matter most to *their* specific use case.
</Callout>
