## 💰 Our Business Model

Understanding how Zeotap generates revenue helps everyone make better decisions — from engineers prioritizing features to salespeople structuring deals to CS managers driving expansion.

### Revenue Model

Zeotap operates as a **B2B SaaS platform** with a combination of:

1. **Platform Subscription Fee** — Annual contract for access to the platform, based on tier and features
2. **Usage-Based Pricing** — Charges that scale with the customer's data volume (profiles, events, destinations)
3. **Professional Services** — Implementation support, custom integrations, training

<Callout type="info" title="Pricing Details">
Specific pricing tiers and rates are confidential. For current pricing, always refer to the latest sales materials or speak with the revenue team.
</Callout>

### Go-To-Market Motion

Zeotap uses a **sales-led GTM strategy**, meaning:

- **Enterprise sales team** drives new business through outbound and inbound
- **Partnerships** with system integrators, agencies, and tech partners extend our reach
- **Events and content** generate inbound interest (conferences, webinars, thought leadership)
- **Customer referrals** from successful implementations

### Growth Levers

How we grow the business:

| Lever | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **New Logos** | Acquiring new enterprise customers |
| **Expansion** | Existing customers adding more data sources, destinations, or regions |
| **Upsell** | Moving customers to higher tiers with more features |
| **Platform Stickiness** | The more data sources connected, the harder it is to switch |
| **Network Effects** | More destinations and integrations attract more customers |

### Unit Economics

Key business metrics everyone should understand:

- **ARR** (Annual Recurring Revenue) — Our total annualized subscription revenue
- **NDR** (Net Dollar Retention) — Revenue from existing customers including expansion, minus churn. Above 100% means existing customers are growing
- **CAC** (Customer Acquisition Cost) — How much it costs to acquire a new customer
- **LTV** (Lifetime Value) — Total revenue expected from a customer over their lifetime
- **Payback Period** — How long it takes to recover the CAC

<Callout type="tip" title="Why This Matters to You">

- **Engineers**: Features that drive expansion (more destinations, better AI) directly impact revenue
- **Sales**: Understanding unit economics helps structure deals that are profitable for both sides
- **CS**: Driving adoption and expansion directly impacts NDR — the most important SaaS metric
- **Marketing**: Creating content and events that attract the right ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) reduces CAC
- **Product**: Prioritizing features that increase stickiness protects revenue long-term

</Callout>
