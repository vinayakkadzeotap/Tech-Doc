## 📊 Health Score Deep Dive

Health scores are the foundation of proactive customer success. This module goes beyond the basics, walking you through the exact calculation methodology, dimension-level thresholds, early warning systems, and intervention playbooks that separate reactive firefighting from strategic account management.

### Health Score Calculation Methodology

The Zeotap Customer Health Score is a composite metric calculated across six weighted dimensions. Each dimension is scored 0-100 independently, then combined using the weights below to produce an overall score.

<StatCard stats={[
  { value: "6", label: "Scoring Dimensions", color: "#3b82f6" },
  { value: "0-100", label: "Score Range", color: "#10b981" },

  { value: "14", label: "Underlying Metrics", color: "#f59e0b" }

The overall health score formula is:

**Health Score = (Product Adoption x 0.30) + (Data Quality x 0.20) + (Engagement x 0.15) + (Business Outcomes x 0.15) + (Support Health x 0.10) + (Relationship Strength x 0.10)**

### Dimension 1: Product Adoption (30%)

Product Adoption is the single most predictive dimension. Customers who actively use the platform renew at 3x the rate of those who do not.

**Metrics and data sources:**
- **Weekly Active Users (WAU):** Unique users logging in per week. Source: platform auth logs.
- **Feature breadth:** Number of distinct product modules used (Audience Builder, Journeys, Destinations, Ada AI, Identity+). Source: product telemetry.
- **Audiences created (last 30 days):** Volume of new audience segments built. Source: audience service.
- **Active destinations:** Number of destinations with a successful sync in the last 7 days. Source: sync logs.

<Callout type="tip" title="Breadth vs. Depth">
A customer creating 50 audiences a month but using only the Audience Builder scores high on volume but low on breadth. Both matter. Breadth signals sticky adoption across the organization, while depth signals power-user engagement. Weight breadth slightly higher in your assessment because it correlates more strongly with retention.
</Callout>

### Dimension 2: Data Quality (20%)

Data quality determines whether the platform can deliver value. Even a highly engaged team will struggle if the underlying data is unreliable.

**Metrics and data sources:**
- **Event volume trend:** Week-over-week change in ingested events. Source: ingestion pipeline.
- **Identity resolution rate:** Percentage of events that resolve to a Unified Customer ID (UCID). Source: identity service.
- **Schema compliance:** Percentage of events matching the configured schema without errors. Source: data validation layer.

    ["Schema compliance", "95%+ events valid", "80-94% events valid", "Below 80% valid", "Validation layer"]
  ]}
  highlightColumn={1}
/>

<Callout type="warning" title="Silent Data Degradation">
Data quality issues are insidious because the customer often does not notice them. A broken SDK tag, a schema change on their end, or a third-party data feed going offline can quietly degrade results for weeks before anyone raises a flag. Build automated alerts for any drop exceeding 20% on any data quality metric.
</Callout>

### Dimension 3: Engagement (15%)

Engagement measures how actively the customer interacts with Zeotap as a company, not just the product.

**Metrics and data sources:**
- **Meeting attendance rate:** Percentage of scheduled CSM meetings actually attended. Source: calendar data / CRM.
- **Email responsiveness:** Average time to respond to CSM outreach. Source: email / CRM.
- **Training participation:** Number of team members who attended webinars, training sessions, or certification programs. Source: LMS / event tracking.

### Dimension 4: Business Outcomes (15%)

Business outcomes are the ultimate measure of whether the customer is achieving their goals. This dimension is partially automated and partially CSM-assessed based on QBR conversations.

**Metrics and data sources:**
- **Use cases delivered:** Number of contracted use cases that are live and producing results. Source: success plan / CRM.
- **Match rates in destinations:** Percentage of audience profiles that match in downstream ad platforms. Source: destination sync reports.
- **Customer-reported ROI:** Revenue lift, ROAS improvement, or efficiency gain attributed to Zeotap. Source: QBR feedback / CRM.

### Dimension 5: Support Health (10%)

Support health captures the reliability of the customer's experience and the effectiveness of issue resolution.

**Metrics and data sources:**
- **Open ticket count:** Number of unresolved support tickets. Source: ticketing system.
- **Average resolution time:** Mean time to resolve tickets (measured against SLA). Source: ticketing system.
- **CSAT on closed tickets:** Customer satisfaction score on resolved tickets. Source: ticketing system surveys.

### Dimension 6: Relationship Strength (10%)

This is the only fully qualitative dimension. The CSM updates it monthly based on their judgment.

**Assessment criteria:**
- **Executive sponsor engagement:** Does the sponsor attend QBRs, respond to strategic outreach, and advocate for the partnership internally?
- **Multi-threading depth:** How many distinct contacts are actively engaged? Is the relationship dependent on a single champion?
- **Sentiment:** Overall tone of interactions. Enthusiastic and proactive? Or distant and transactional?

  highlightColumn={1}

<Callout type="important" title="Update Relationship Scores After Key Events">
Do not wait for the monthly cycle to update relationship strength. If a champion leaves, an executive escalation occurs, or sentiment shifts dramatically during a call, update the score immediately. Stale relationship data is worse than no data because it creates a false sense of security.
</Callout>

### Early Warning Indicators: Leading vs. Lagging

Understanding the difference between leading and lagging indicators is essential for proactive CS. Leading indicators give you time to intervene. Lagging indicators tell you it is already too late.

<Callout type="tip" title="Build Your Early Warning System">
Configure automated alerts for these leading indicator thresholds: WAU drops below 2 for any account, event volume declines more than 20% WoW, no audience created in 21+ days, two consecutive meeting cancellations. Each alert should trigger a specific playbook, not just a notification.
</Callout>

### Intervention Playbooks by Health Tier

<Expandable title="Tier 1: Healthy (Score 80-100) — Grow and Advocate" defaultOpen={true}>

Healthy accounts are your growth engine. The goal is expansion, not maintenance.

**Weekly actions:**
- Monitor for expansion signals (volume growth, new use case requests, new team interest)
- Ensure training resources are available for new users

**Monthly actions:**
- Review usage trends for upsell timing
- Identify case study and referral candidates
- Introduce new features relevant to their use cases

**Quarterly actions:**
- Conduct full QBR with executive sponsor
- Present ROI summary and next-quarter success plan
- Propose expansion where appropriate

**Key metrics to watch:** Feature breadth (are they using everything they pay for?), team growth (new users onboarding?), event volume trend (growing = expansion ready).

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Tier 2: At-Risk (Score 50-79) — Diagnose and Stabilize" defaultOpen={false}>

At-risk accounts require focused attention to prevent further decline. The goal is to identify the root cause and build a recovery plan within 2 weeks.

**Immediate actions (within 48 hours of score entering this tier):**
- Review all six dimensions to identify which are dragging the score down
- Pull the last 30 days of activity logs and support tickets
- Schedule a call with your primary contact to discuss the state of the partnership

**Week 1 actions:**
- Document root causes for each underperforming dimension
- Create a 30/60/90-day recovery plan with specific milestones
- Share the plan with the customer and get their buy-in

**Week 2-4 actions:**
- Increase meeting cadence to bi-weekly
- Execute the recovery plan: training sessions, data health audits, use case workshops
- Provide weekly progress updates to the customer

**Ongoing until restored to Healthy:**
- Review health score weekly with your manager
- Flag any further decline immediately
- Consider involving a solutions consultant for technical issues

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Tier 3: Critical (Score 0-49) — Escalate and Save" defaultOpen={false}>

Critical accounts are at immediate churn risk. Every day without action increases the probability of loss.

**Immediate actions (within 24 hours):**
- Escalate to CS leadership (VP or Director level)
- Schedule an emergency internal review: CSM + manager + solutions consultant
- Draft an executive-to-executive outreach email for your leader to send

**Week 1 actions:**
- Conduct a joint call with customer leadership and Zeotap leadership
- Present a "Get Well" plan with aggressive timelines and Zeotap investment (dedicated SE time, training, data audit)
- Address all open support tickets as P1 priority

**Week 2-4 actions:**
- Assign a dedicated solutions consultant for hands-on support
- Run daily standups internally until the account stabilizes
- Deliver quick wins to rebuild confidence (fix a broken integration, launch a new audience, improve match rates)

**If renewal is within 90 days:**
- Engage sales leadership and finance for potential concession strategy
- Prepare competitive defense materials if applicable
- Consider a short-term contract extension to buy time for recovery

</Expandable>

### Weekly and Monthly Health Review Cadence

<Expandable title="Weekly Health Review (CSM, 30 minutes)" defaultOpen={true}>

**Every Monday morning, review your full portfolio:**

1. **Sort by score, ascending.** Start with your most critical accounts.
2. **Check score changes.** Any account that moved more than 10 points in either direction needs investigation.
3. **Review automated alerts.** Clear or act on any triggered alerts from the early warning system.
4. **Update relationship scores.** If you had meaningful interactions last week, update the qualitative assessment.
5. **Plan the week.** Identify the 3-5 most important customer actions for the week based on health data.

**Output:** Updated health notes in CRM, weekly priorities documented.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Monthly Health Review (CSM + Manager, 45 minutes)" defaultOpen={false}>

**First week of every month, hold a portfolio review with your manager:**

1. **Portfolio snapshot.** Total accounts by health tier (green/yellow/red), changes from last month.
2. **Deep-dive on at-risk and critical accounts.** Review recovery plans, timeline, and progress.
3. **Expansion pipeline.** Which healthy accounts are expansion-ready? What is the estimated expansion value?
4. **Renewal forecast.** For renewals in the next 120 days: health score, risk assessment, and confidence level (high/medium/low).
5. **Resource requests.** Does any account need a solutions consultant, executive engagement, or additional training investment?

**Output:** Updated forecast in CRM, action items with owners and due dates, escalations raised.

</Expandable>

Use benchmarks as aspirational targets, not rigid pass/fail thresholds.

### Common Health Score Pitfalls

<Expandable title="Pitfall 1: Over-Reliance on Automated Scores" defaultOpen={false}>

Automated scores can miss context. A customer migrating to a new website will have temporarily low event volumes. A customer in a seasonal business will have cyclical usage patterns. Always pair quantitative scores with qualitative context. Build a "context notes" field in your health tracking to capture these nuances.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Pitfall 2: Gaming the Relationship Score" defaultOpen={false}>

CSMs sometimes inflate the relationship score because it feels subjective and there is no automated check. Combat this by requiring specific evidence for each rating: "Score is 80 because the executive sponsor attended the last 2 QBRs and proactively shared their Q3 priorities." Vague justifications like "things feel fine" should trigger a manager conversation.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Pitfall 3: Ignoring Healthy Accounts" defaultOpen={false}>

It is tempting to spend all your time on red and yellow accounts. But neglecting green accounts creates future reds. Allocate at least 20% of your weekly time to proactive engagement with healthy accounts: feature introductions, expansion conversations, and relationship deepening. The best time to grow an account is when everything is going well.

</Expandable>
