## 💚 Account Health Scores

A health score is a composite metric that tells you — at a glance — whether a customer account is thriving, coasting, or at risk. This module explains what goes into a health score, how to interpret signals, and when to act.

### Pillar 1: Product Usage

Product usage is the strongest leading indicator of account health. Track these metrics:

<ComparisonTable
  headers={["Metric", "Healthy", "Warning", "Critical"]}
  rows={[
    ["Weekly active users", "3+ users logging in", "1-2 users logging in", "No logins in 2+ weeks"],
    ["Audiences created (last 30d)", "5+ new audiences", "1-4 new audiences", "Zero new audiences"],
    ["Journeys active", "2+ running journeys", "1 running journey", "No active journeys"],
    ["Destinations syncing", "3+ active destinations", "1-2 active destinations", "No syncs in 7+ days"],
    ["Zoe AI queries", "Regular usage", "Occasional usage", "Never used"]
  ]}
/>

<Callout type="tip" title="Feature Adoption Matters">
A customer using only the Audience Builder but not Journeys or Zoe AI is underutilizing the platform. This is an expansion opportunity but also a risk — they may conclude the platform does not justify the cost. Proactively introduce underused features.
</Callout>

### Pillar 2: Data Health

Without good data flowing in, nothing else works. Monitor these signals:

**Event Volume Trends**
- **Healthy:** Stable or growing event volume week-over-week
- **Warning:** Volume dropped 20%+ from the trailing 4-week average
- **Critical:** Volume dropped 50%+ or went to zero

**Identity Resolution Rate**
- **Healthy:** 60%+ of events resolve to a known UCID
- **Warning:** 30-60% resolution rate
- **Critical:** Below 30% — likely missing identity fields or SDK misconfiguration

**Data Freshness**
- **Healthy:** Most recent event within the last hour
- **Warning:** No events in 6-24 hours
- **Critical:** No events in 24+ hours

<Callout type="warning" title="Volume Drops Are Often Silent">
Customers frequently do not notice when their event volume drops — especially if it is caused by a website deploy that removed the SDK snippet. Set up internal alerts for any account where event volume drops more than 30% day-over-day and proactively notify the customer.
</Callout>

Support interactions tell you about both product stability and customer sentiment.

**Red flag pattern:** A customer who stops filing support tickets after a period of high ticket volume may have given up, not gotten better. Check their usage metrics before assuming silence is good news.

### Pillar 4: Relationship Health

This pillar is qualitative and requires your judgment as a CSM.

**Positive signals:**
- Executive sponsor attends QBRs and engages with results
- Customer proactively shares roadmap and asks about upcoming features
- Multiple team members are trained and active on the platform
- Customer references Zeotap positively in industry conversations
- They respond to emails and meeting invites promptly

**Negative signals:**
- Key champion leaves the company (critical risk event)
- Meeting cancellations increasing
- Emails go unanswered for 5+ business days
- Customer pushes back on QBR scheduling
- New stakeholders are brought in who were not part of the original deal

<Expandable title="What to Do When Your Champion Leaves" defaultOpen={false}>

Losing your primary champion is one of the highest-risk events in CS. Follow this protocol:

1. **Within 24 hours:** Reach out to the executive sponsor to understand the transition plan
2. **Within 1 week:** Get introduced to the replacement contact
3. **Within 2 weeks:** Schedule an onboarding session for the new contact — treat them like a new customer
4. **Within 30 days:** Re-establish the meeting cadence and re-present the value delivered so far
5. **Ongoing:** Ensure at least 2-3 contacts are engaged so you are never single-threaded

Document the outgoing champion's context: what use cases they cared about, what success looked like to them, and any open issues.

</Expandable>

---

### Pillar 5: Business Outcome

Ultimately, health comes down to whether the customer is achieving their goals.

**Outcome metrics to track:**
- **Audience reach:** Are activated audiences reaching enough users in downstream platforms?
- **Match rates:** What percentage of Zeotap profiles match in destinations like Google Ads or Meta?
- **Campaign performance:** Are campaigns using Zeotap audiences outperforming others?
- **Time savings:** Has the platform reduced manual data work for the marketing team?
- **Data unification:** How many profiles have been unified across channels?

### Calculating the Composite Score

While exact scoring models vary by organization, here is a reference framework:

<ComparisonTable
  headers={["Pillar", "Weight", "Score Range", "How to Measure"]}
  rows={[
    ["Product Usage", "30%", "0-100", "Automated from platform telemetry"],
    ["Data Health", "25%", "0-100", "Automated from pipeline metrics"],
    ["Support Health", "15%", "0-100", "Automated from ticketing system"],
    ["Relationship", "15%", "0-100", "CSM subjective assessment (updated monthly)"],
    ["Business Outcome", "15%", "0-100", "QBR data + customer feedback"]
  ]}
/>

**Overall score interpretation:**
- **80-100 (Green):** Healthy account, focus on expansion
- **60-79 (Yellow):** Needs attention, identify and address gaps
- **40-59 (Orange):** At risk, create an action plan within 1 week
- **0-39 (Red):** Critical risk, escalate to CS leadership immediately

### Reading the Health Dashboard

When you open the health dashboard in your CS tool, focus on these views:

<Expandable title="Daily Review Checklist" defaultOpen={false}>

Spend 10 minutes each morning reviewing your portfolio:

1. **Sort by health score** (lowest first) — who needs attention today?
2. **Check for score changes** — any account that dropped 10+ points in the last week needs investigation
3. **Review open P1/P2 tickets** — are any of your accounts waiting on critical fixes?
4. **Check event volume alerts** — did any account have a data drop overnight?
5. **Review upcoming renewals** (next 90 days) — are those accounts healthy?

</Expandable>

<Callout type="important" title="Health Scores Are a Starting Point, Not the Answer">
A health score tells you where to look, not what to do. A green account can still churn if a new CTO decides to switch platforms. A red account can be saved with the right executive engagement. Always combine quantitative scores with your qualitative understanding of the account.
</Callout>

---

### Acting on Health Score Changes

| Score Trend | Action |
|---|---|
| Green and stable | Focus on expansion opportunities, schedule QBR |
| Green dropping to Yellow | Investigate root cause within 48 hours, reach out to champion |
| Yellow and stable | Create improvement plan, increase meeting cadence to bi-weekly |
| Yellow dropping to Red | Escalate to CS leadership, schedule emergency call with customer |
| Red and stable | Executive-level intervention, consider involving product team |
| Any score, renewal in < 90 days | Treat as highest priority regardless of color |
