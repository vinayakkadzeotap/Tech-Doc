### Retail

<Expandable title="Retail: Key Use Cases" defaultOpen={true}>

1. **Omnichannel identity unification:** Merge online browsing, app activity, POS transactions, and loyalty program data into a single customer view.
2. **Seasonal campaign audiences:** Build high-intent audiences for Black Friday, holiday season, back-to-school, and clearance events.
3. **Loyalty program segmentation:** Segment by loyalty tier, purchase frequency, average basket size, and churn risk.
4. **Lookalike expansion:** Find new customers who resemble your best buyers using Zeotap's lookalike modeling.
5. **Abandoned cart journeys:** Trigger multi-channel win-back journeys for cart abandoners within 1-4 hours.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Retail: Onboarding Priorities" defaultOpen={false}>

**Week 1-2:** Connect POS data, e-commerce event stream, and loyalty database. Configure identity resolution across email, phone, and device ID.

**Week 3-4:** Build the first 5 audiences: high-value customers, lapsed buyers (90+ days), cart abandoners, loyalty tier upgrades, and seasonal shoppers.

**Week 5-6:** Activate audiences to Google Ads, Meta, email platform, and push notifications. Validate match rates.

**Week 7-8:** Launch first automated journey (abandoned cart or loyalty win-back). Measure initial results.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Retail: Common Pitfalls" defaultOpen={false}>

- **POS data quality:** In-store transaction data is often messy. Expect 2-3 iterations to get the schema and identity matching right.
- **Consent fragmentation:** Retail customers may have separate consent records across web, app, and in-store. Ensure unified consent management before activation.
- **Seasonal urgency:** Retail stakeholders expect the CDP to be fully operational before their peak season. If you miss the setup window, the customer may not see value for 6-12 months.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Retail: Success Metrics & KPI Dashboard" defaultOpen={false}>

Configure these KPIs in the customer's dashboard:
- **Unified profile rate:** % of known customers with cross-channel identity linkage
- **Audience activation reach:** Total unique customers reached across all channels
- **Campaign ROAS:** Return on ad spend for CDP-powered audiences vs. control
- **Cart abandonment recovery rate:** % of abandoned carts recovered via journey
- **Loyalty program engagement:** % of loyalty members in an active CDP segment

</Expandable>

### BFSI (Banking, Financial Services, Insurance)

<Expandable title="BFSI: Key Use Cases" defaultOpen={true}>

1. **Consent-first activation:** Activate customer segments while maintaining strict GDPR/CCPA/local regulatory compliance with full audit trails.
2. **Product cross-sell modeling:** Identify customers likely to adopt a new financial product (credit card, mortgage, insurance policy) based on behavioral and transactional signals.
3. **Churn prevention for high-value accounts:** Build predictive audiences of customers showing early signs of attrition (reduced transaction frequency, balance decline).
4. **KYC-enriched profiles:** Unify CRM, transactional, and digital behavior data while respecting KYC and data residency requirements.
5. **Lifecycle marketing automation:** Automate onboarding journeys for new account holders, annual review reminders, and product maturity notifications.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="BFSI: Onboarding Priorities" defaultOpen={false}>

**Week 1-2:** Compliance configuration first. Set up data residency, consent management rules, and encryption. Engage the customer's legal/compliance team early.

**Week 3-4:** Ingest CRM and transactional data. Identity resolution using email, phone, and account ID. Validate no PII leaks in event payloads.

**Week 5-8:** Build initial segments: high-value customers, cross-sell candidates, at-risk accounts. Activate to approved channels only (typically email, mobile banking notifications).

**Week 9-12:** Launch first journey (onboarding or cross-sell). Present compliance audit report to the customer's legal team.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="BFSI: Common Pitfalls" defaultOpen={false}>

- **Compliance paralysis:** BFSI stakeholders may delay implementation indefinitely due to compliance concerns. Get their legal team involved in week 1, not week 6.
- **Data residency misconfiguration:** Ensure the Zeotap instance is deployed in the correct region. Moving data after the fact is extremely painful.
- **Slow approvals:** Every audience activation may require internal approval. Build approval workflows into the journey design so the customer does not blame Zeotap for delays.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="BFSI: Success Metrics & KPI Dashboard" defaultOpen={false}>

- **Compliance audit pass rate:** 100% of activated audiences compliant with consent rules
- **Cross-sell conversion rate:** % of targeted customers who adopted a new product
- **Customer lifetime value growth:** Average CLV change for CDP-managed segments
- **Onboarding journey completion rate:** % of new customers completing the welcome journey
- **Data residency compliance:** 100% of data stored in approved regions

</Expandable>

### Telco

<Expandable title="Telco: Key Use Cases" defaultOpen={true}>

1. **Predictive churn prevention:** Identify subscribers at risk of churning based on usage decline, support call patterns, and contract end dates.
2. **Plan upgrade targeting:** Build audiences of subscribers likely to benefit from (and accept) a plan upgrade based on usage patterns that exceed their current tier.
3. **Win-back campaigns:** Re-engage former subscribers with tailored offers based on their historical usage profile and reason for leaving.
4. **Household-level unification:** Link individual subscriber lines to household accounts for family plan and bundle opportunities.
5. **Network experience correlation:** Connect network quality data (coverage complaints, speed tests) with subscriber behavior to identify experience-driven churn risk.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Telco: Onboarding Priorities" defaultOpen={false}>

**Week 1-2:** Ingest subscriber data (CRM, billing, usage logs). Identity resolution across subscriber ID, device ID, and household.

**Week 3-4:** Build churn risk model using Zeotap's predictive traits. Validate against historical churn data.

**Week 5-6:** Create first audiences: churn risk (high/medium/low), upgrade candidates, win-back pool. Activate to email, SMS, and call center platforms.

**Week 7-8:** Launch automated win-back journey. Set up proactive retention triggers for high-value subscribers showing early churn signals.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Telco: Common Pitfalls" defaultOpen={false}>

- **Massive data volumes:** Telco event streams are enormous (network events, call records). Work with the customer to filter to behavioral events that matter for marketing, not raw network telemetry.
- **Siloed departments:** Marketing, network ops, and customer care often have separate data stores. The CDP integration needs buy-in from multiple teams.
- **Contract timing blindness:** If you do not ingest contract end dates, you miss the most important churn predictor. Make this a day-1 data requirement.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Telco: Success Metrics & KPI Dashboard" defaultOpen={false}>

- **Churn rate reduction:** % decrease in monthly subscriber churn for targeted segments
- **Win-back conversion rate:** % of former subscribers who reactivate
- **Plan upgrade acceptance rate:** % of targeted subscribers who accept upgrade offers
- **Subscriber lifetime value:** Average revenue per subscriber for CDP-managed cohorts
- **Time to intervention:** Average days between churn signal detection and first outreach

</Expandable>

### Media

<Expandable title="Media: Key Use Cases" defaultOpen={true}>

1. **First-party audience monetization:** Package first-party reader/viewer segments for direct-sold advertising deals.
2. **Content personalization audiences:** Build segments based on content consumption patterns (topic affinity, reading depth, frequency) for editorial personalization.
3. **Subscription conversion modeling:** Identify anonymous visitors most likely to convert to paid subscribers based on engagement behavior.
4. **Advertiser-ready segment packaging:** Create standardized audience taxonomies (demographics, interests, purchase intent) that advertisers can activate programmatically.
5. **Cross-property unification:** Unify audience data across multiple media properties (web, app, newsletter, podcast) into a single identity graph.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Media: Onboarding Priorities" defaultOpen={false}>

**Week 1-2:** Implement event tracking across all digital properties. Configure identity resolution for authenticated (subscriber) and anonymous (cookie/device ID) users.

**Week 3-4:** Build content affinity taxonomy. Create initial audience segments: subscriber lookalikes, high-engagement readers, topic-based cohorts.

**Week 5-6:** Package advertiser-ready segments. Connect to programmatic platforms (DV360, The Trade Desk) and validate delivery.

**Week 7-8:** Launch subscription conversion journey for high-propensity anonymous visitors.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Media: Common Pitfalls" defaultOpen={false}>

- **Cookie deprecation anxiety:** Media customers are especially sensitive to identity changes. Position Zeotap's first-party approach and ID+ as the solution, not the problem.
- **Low authenticated rates:** Many media properties have less than 15% authenticated traffic. Focus on progressive identity collection strategies alongside CDP implementation.
- **Revenue attribution complexity:** Tying CDP-powered audiences to ad revenue is hard. Work with the customer's ad ops team to set up A/B tests from day one.

</Expandable>

### Gaming

<Expandable title="Gaming: Key Use Cases" defaultOpen={true}>

1. **Player lifecycle management:** Segment players by lifecycle stage (new, active, whale, lapsing, churned) and trigger stage-appropriate engagement.
2. **In-game event monetization:** Build audiences based on in-game behavior (level reached, items purchased, time played) for targeted offers.
3. **Lapse prevention journeys:** Detect players whose session frequency or duration is declining and trigger re-engagement via push, email, or in-game messaging.
4. **Cross-title unification:** For publishers with multiple games, unify player identities across titles to understand total engagement and cross-promote.
5. **Whale identification and nurturing:** Identify high-spending players early and create VIP engagement programs to maximize lifetime value.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Gaming: Onboarding Priorities" defaultOpen={false}>

**Week 1-2:** Integrate game client SDK for event streaming. Configure player identity resolution (game account ID, device ID, platform account).

**Week 3-4:** Build lifecycle stage segments. Define "active," "lapsing," and "churned" thresholds based on the game's natural engagement cadence.

**Week 5-6:** Activate player segments to push notification service, email, and in-game messaging system.

**Week 7-8:** Launch lapse prevention journey. A/B test different re-engagement offers (bonus currency, limited-time event, social challenge).

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Gaming: Common Pitfalls" defaultOpen={false}>

- **Event volume spikes:** Game launches and live events create massive event volume spikes. Ensure the contracted volume accommodates these peaks.
- **Privacy regulations for minors:** Many games have underage players. COPPA and similar regulations require careful consent management and data handling.
- **Fast engagement cycles:** Gamers churn much faster than other verticals. A "lapsing" player in gaming might be someone who has not played in 3 days, not 30 days. Calibrate thresholds accordingly.

</Expandable>

### SaaS

<Expandable title="SaaS: Key Use Cases" defaultOpen={true}>

1. **Product-qualified lead (PQL) scoring:** Build audiences of trial or freemium users whose product usage signals readiness for a sales conversation.
2. **Expansion signal audiences:** Identify accounts whose usage is growing beyond their current plan for upsell targeting.
3. **Churn risk segmentation:** Detect accounts with declining product engagement and trigger CS or automated outreach.
4. **Account-level unification:** Unify individual user behavior into account-level profiles for B2B targeting.
5. **Onboarding completion optimization:** Build journeys that nudge new users through the activation milestones that correlate with long-term retention.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="SaaS: Onboarding Priorities" defaultOpen={false}>

**Week 1-2:** Ingest product usage events from the customer's application (API calls, feature usage, login events). Configure account-level identity resolution.

**Week 3-4:** Build PQL scoring model based on activation milestones. Create segments: trial users, activated users, at-risk accounts, expansion candidates.

**Week 5-6:** Activate segments to CRM (Salesforce/HubSpot), email platform, and sales engagement tools.

**Week 7-8:** Launch onboarding nudge journey for new trial users. Measure activation rate improvement.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="SaaS: Common Pitfalls" defaultOpen={false}>

- **Account vs. user confusion:** SaaS customers think in accounts (companies), not individuals. Ensure the CDP is configured for account-level aggregation from day one.
- **Product data complexity:** SaaS product event schemas can be deeply nested and high-volume. Work with the customer's engineering team to define the minimum viable event set for marketing use cases.
- **Attribution to pipeline:** SaaS customers want to see CDP impact on pipeline and revenue. Set up CRM integration early so you can track audience-to-pipeline attribution.

</Expandable>

<Callout type="tip" title="Plan QBRs Around Seasonal Peaks">
Schedule QBRs 4-6 weeks before a customer's peak season, not during it. This gives you time to help them prepare campaigns and audiences, and it positions you as proactive rather than reactive. A QBR in early October for a retail customer is far more valuable than one in late November.
</Callout>

### Stakeholder Mapping by Vertical

<Expandable title="Who to Engage and Their Motivations" defaultOpen={true}>

**Retail:**
- **CMO/VP Marketing:** Wants revenue attribution and campaign performance. Speak in ROAS and conversion rate.
- **Head of CRM/Loyalty:** Wants unified customer view and personalization. Speak in engagement lift and retention.
- **E-commerce Director:** Wants on-site personalization and audience reach. Speak in traffic quality and conversion.
- **IT/Data Engineering:** Wants clean integration and low maintenance. Speak in API reliability and data quality.

**BFSI:**
- **Chief Data Officer:** Wants compliant data unification. Speak in data governance and regulatory audit readiness.
- **VP Digital Marketing:** Wants customer acquisition and cross-sell. Speak in cost per acquisition and product adoption rate.
- **Compliance Officer:** Wants zero regulatory risk. Speak in consent management, audit trails, and data residency.
- **Head of Customer Experience:** Wants journey personalization. Speak in NPS and customer effort score.

**Telco:**
- **VP Customer Value Management:** Wants reduced churn and increased ARPU. Speak in churn rate reduction and plan upgrade conversion.
- **Head of Marketing Ops:** Wants campaign efficiency. Speak in audience reach and campaign execution speed.
- **Network Quality Team:** Wants experience-driven insights. Speak in network satisfaction correlation.
- **Digital Transformation Lead:** Wants data modernization. Speak in time-to-insight and operational efficiency.

**Media:**
- **Chief Revenue Officer:** Wants ad revenue growth. Speak in CPM lift and sell-through rate.
- **VP Audience Development:** Wants subscriber growth. Speak in conversion rate and engaged reader metrics.
- **Head of Ad Operations:** Wants audience packaging and delivery. Speak in fill rate and audience addressability.
- **Editor-in-Chief:** Wants content personalization. Speak in engagement depth and return visit rate.

**Gaming:**
- **Head of Live Ops:** Wants player engagement and monetization. Speak in DAU/MAU, session length, and ARPDAU.
- **VP Marketing:** Wants user acquisition and re-engagement. Speak in CPI, retention curves, and reactivation rate.
- **Product Manager:** Wants behavioral insights. Speak in feature adoption and player progression.
- **Analytics Lead:** Wants data infrastructure. Speak in event pipeline reliability and query speed.

**SaaS:**
- **VP Growth/Marketing:** Wants pipeline and revenue. Speak in PQL conversion and pipeline attribution.
- **VP Customer Success:** Wants retention and expansion. Speak in NRR and health scores.
- **Head of Product:** Wants usage insights. Speak in feature adoption and activation rates.
- **RevOps Leader:** Wants system integration and data flow. Speak in CRM sync reliability and data quality.

</Expandable>
