## 📋 QBR & Executive Review Template

The Quarterly Business Review is your highest-leverage touchpoint with a customer. A great QBR reinforces value, deepens relationships, surfaces expansion opportunities, and resets expectations. A bad QBR feels like a sales pitch or a status update that could have been an email. This module gives you a complete, repeatable framework for QBRs that drive outcomes.

### Data to Pull Before Every QBR

<Expandable title="Data Collection Checklist" defaultOpen={true}>

**Platform Usage (from product telemetry):**
- Weekly active users (trend over the quarter)
- Features used and frequency (Audience Builder, Journeys, Ada AI, Destinations)
- Audiences created this quarter (count, types, activation channels)
- Active destinations and sync success rates
- Event volume trend (monthly, with WoW granularity)

**Health Score (from CS platform):**
- Current overall score and per-dimension breakdown
- Score trend over the quarter (improving, stable, declining)
- Any alerts triggered during the quarter

**Support Tickets (from ticketing system):**
- Total tickets opened this quarter
- Severity distribution (P1/P2/P3/P4)
- Average resolution time vs. SLA
- CSAT on closed tickets
- Any unresolved tickets (list with status and expected resolution)

**ROI Metrics (from customer data + QBR conversations):**
- Audience reach: total unique profiles activated this quarter
- Destination match rates (average and per-destination)
- Campaign performance: ROAS, conversion rate, or engagement lift attributed to Zeotap audiences
- Time savings: estimated hours saved by the marketing team per week
- Profile unification rate: % of profiles with cross-channel identity linkage

**Business Context (from CRM + your notes):**
- Renewal date and contract terms
- Open expansion opportunities
- Stakeholder changes (new hires, departures, reorgs)
- Strategic priorities the customer shared last quarter
- Action items from the previous QBR (completed vs. outstanding)

</Expandable>

### Complete QBR Deck Structure: 10 Slides

<Expandable title="Slide 1: Title & Agenda (1 minute)" defaultOpen={true}>

**Purpose:** Set the stage, confirm the agenda, and establish a professional tone.

**Content:**
- Customer logo + Zeotap logo
- QBR date and quarter covered
- Attendee names and roles (from both sides)
- Agenda overview (time-boxed sections)
- "Anything you want to add to the agenda before we begin?"

**Tip:** Always ask if they want to add agenda items. This shows you value their time and surfaces hidden concerns early.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Slide 2: Executive Summary (3 minutes)" defaultOpen={true}>

**Purpose:** Give the senior stakeholders the headlines so they get value even if they drop off early.

**Content:**
- 3-4 bullet point summary of the quarter: what went well, what needs attention, and what is next
- Overall health score with color indicator
- One sentence on ROI delivered
- One sentence on the key recommendation for next quarter

**Template:**

> "This quarter, [Customer Name] achieved [key outcome]. Platform adoption is [strong/growing/needs attention], with [X] audiences activated across [Y] channels. We delivered an estimated [ROI metric]. Looking ahead, we recommend [key initiative] to drive [expected outcome]."

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Slide 3: Goals Review — Last Quarter (5 minutes)" defaultOpen={false}>

**Purpose:** Accountability. Review the goals set in the previous QBR and report on progress.

**Content:**
- Table with 3-5 goals from last QBR
- Status for each: Completed, In Progress, Blocked, Not Started
- Brief explanation for any goal not completed
- Link to action items from the previous QBR follow-up email

**Important:** Never skip this slide. If you set goals last quarter and never follow up, the customer learns that QBR commitments are empty. Always close the loop.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Slide 4: Value Delivered — Metrics (7 minutes)" defaultOpen={false}>

**Purpose:** This is the most important slide in the deck. Prove the platform is delivering value.

**Content:**
- Key metrics with quarter-over-quarter trends
- Visualize: audiences created, profiles unified, campaigns activated, match rates
- If possible, tie platform metrics to business outcomes: "Your Zeotap-powered audiences drove a 28% higher ROAS than non-CDP audiences in Q3."
- Include a before/after comparison if this is the first full quarter of usage

**Presentation approach:**
- Lead with the metric the customer cares about most (ask yourself: what would their CMO want to see?)
- Use absolute numbers, not just percentages: "2.3M unique profiles activated" is more impressive than "12% increase"
- If a metric declined, acknowledge it proactively and explain why

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Slide 5: Platform Usage Review (5 minutes)" defaultOpen={false}>

**Purpose:** Show what features are being used, who is using them, and where there are adoption gaps.

**Content:**
- Feature usage heatmap: which modules are heavily used, lightly used, or unused
- User activity: active users by team/role
- Highlight "power users" and suggest they could train others
- Identify unused features and recommend specific use cases

**Why this matters for expansion:** If the customer sees they are only using 3 of 6 available modules, you can naturally ask: "Would it be helpful if we did a workshop on Journeys? Customers in your industry are seeing strong results with automated re-engagement."

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Slide 6: Data Health & Quality (3 minutes)" defaultOpen={false}>

**Purpose:** Ensure the foundation is solid. Data issues undermine everything else.

**Content:**
- Event volume trend (chart showing monthly volume)
- Identity resolution rate (current vs. last quarter)
- Schema compliance rate
- Any data incidents during the quarter and their resolution

**Keep it brief.** Unless there are issues, this slide should take 2-3 minutes. If there are data quality problems, allocate more time and bring a solutions consultant.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Slide 7: Support Summary (3 minutes)" defaultOpen={false}>

**Purpose:** Demonstrate that issues are being resolved and the experience is improving.

**Content:**
- Tickets opened vs. resolved this quarter
- Average resolution time vs. SLA
- CSAT trend
- Any outstanding tickets with expected resolution timeline

**If support was rough this quarter:** Do not hide it. Acknowledge the issues, explain root causes, and present what has been done to prevent recurrence. Customers respect honesty far more than spin.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Slide 8: Roadmap Preview (5 minutes)" defaultOpen={false}>

**Purpose:** Get the customer excited about what is coming and show that Zeotap is investing in their success.

**Content:**
- 3-5 upcoming features or enhancements relevant to this customer's use cases
- Expected availability timelines (quarter-level, not specific dates)
- How each feature connects to their goals

**Rules:**
- Only show roadmap items relevant to this customer. A generic product roadmap is a wasted slide.
- Never promise dates you cannot keep. Use "targeted for Q3" rather than "shipping in July."
- If a customer requested a feature that is now on the roadmap, call it out: "You asked for this in our last QBR — it is coming in Q2."

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Slide 9: Strategic Recommendations (5 minutes)" defaultOpen={false}>

**Purpose:** Position yourself as a strategic advisor. This is where expansion opportunities live.

**Content:**
- 2-3 recommendations for next quarter, each tied to a specific business outcome
- For each recommendation: what to do, why it matters, and expected impact
- One recommendation can be an expansion opportunity, but frame it as advice, not a pitch

**Example recommendations:**
1. "Launch a cart abandonment journey to recover an estimated $200K in lost revenue per quarter."
2. "Onboard your analytics team (3 additional seats) so they can self-serve audience insights instead of waiting for marketing."
3. "Upgrade to the next volume tier before you hit your cap in 6 weeks — this reduces your per-profile cost by 28%."

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Slide 10: Next Quarter Goals & Action Items (3 minutes)" defaultOpen={false}>

**Purpose:** Close with clear commitments from both sides.

**Content:**
- 3-5 goals for next quarter (specific, measurable, with owners)
- Action items from this QBR with owners and due dates
- Next QBR date (schedule it before leaving the meeting)

**Template:**

| Goal | Owner | Due Date | Success Metric |
|------|-------|----------|----------------|
| Launch abandoned cart journey | Customer marketing + Zeotap SE | End of Q2 | Journey live, 500+ triggers/week |
| Onboard analytics team | Zeotap CSM | Mid-Q2 | 3 analysts trained and active |
| Achieve 75%+ identity resolution | Zeotap SE + Customer data eng | End of Q2 | Resolution rate in dashboard |

</Expandable>

### Presenting Value Delivered vs. Investment

This is the slide that justifies the contract and makes renewal a foregone conclusion. Here is how to structure the value argument.

<ArchDiagram
  title="Value Presentation Framework"
  steps={[
    { label: "Investment", detail: "State the annual contract value clearly. Do not avoid the number.", icon: "💰" },
    { label: "Value Delivered", detail: "Quantify: revenue generated, costs saved, time recovered, risks mitigated.", icon: "📊" },
    { label: "ROI Multiple", detail: "Calculate: Value / Investment = ROI. Target 3-5x minimum.", icon: "📈" },
    { label: "Intangible Value", detail: "Highlight: team capability growth, data maturity, competitive advantage.", icon: "🧠" },
    { label: "Forward Value", detail: "Project: What additional value is achievable next quarter with current or expanded scope.", icon: "🔮" }
  ]}
/>

<Callout type="tip" title="The ROI Slide Is Not Optional">
Even if the customer has not explicitly asked for ROI justification, present it anyway. The executive sponsor needs ammunition to defend the budget internally. Give them a slide they can forward to their CFO. Make it simple: "For every $1 invested in Zeotap, you generated $X in measurable value."
</Callout>

**If ROI is hard to quantify:**
- Use efficiency metrics: "Your team saves 12 hours per week on audience building, equivalent to $X/year in labor."
- Use competitive benchmarks: "Your match rates are 15 points above the industry average, translating to broader reach at the same ad spend."
- Use risk avoidance: "Consent management through Zeotap has prevented an estimated $X in potential regulatory fines."

<Callout type="important" title="The 80/20 Rule">
80% of QBR time should be about the customer's success. 20% can be forward-looking. If the customer leaves feeling the meeting was about them, they will be open to your recommendations. If they leave feeling it was about your revenue targets, trust erodes.
</Callout>

### Handling Difficult QBRs

Not every QBR goes smoothly. Here are playbooks for the three most common difficult scenarios.

<Expandable title="Scenario 1: Declining Usage" defaultOpen={true}>

**The situation:** Platform usage has dropped significantly. Fewer logins, fewer audiences, fewer syncs.

**Do not:** Gloss over it, blame the customer, or pretend the data does not exist.

**Do:**
1. **Acknowledge it directly:** "Usage declined this quarter, and I want to understand why."
2. **Present the data honestly:** Show the usage trend and ask what changed on their side.
3. **Listen for root causes:** Common reasons include staff turnover, budget cuts, competing priorities, or product frustration.
4. **Propose a recovery plan:** "Here is what I recommend to get back on track: a 2-hour workshop to retrain the team, a simplified use case to rebuild momentum, and bi-weekly check-ins for the next 6 weeks."
5. **Get commitment:** "Can we agree on these 3 actions and revisit in 30 days?"

**Key mindset:** Declining usage is a symptom, not the disease. Your job is to diagnose the root cause, not just present the numbers.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Scenario 2: Unresolved Support Issues" defaultOpen={false}>

**The situation:** The customer has had persistent support issues that were not fully resolved.

**Do not:** Deflect blame to other teams or minimize the impact.

**Do:**
1. **Lead with accountability:** "I know support has not met your expectations this quarter. Let me walk through what happened and what we are doing about it."
2. **Present a ticket-by-ticket summary:** For each unresolved or delayed ticket, show the current status, root cause, and expected resolution.
3. **Explain systemic changes:** If changes were made (e.g., dedicated support engineer assigned, escalation path updated), present them as concrete actions.
4. **Ask what "good" looks like:** "What would need to be true for you to feel confident in our support going forward?"
5. **Follow through:** Whatever you commit to in this conversation, deliver it. Broken promises after a difficult QBR are relationship-ending.

</Expandable>

<Expandable title="Scenario 3: Competitive Threat" defaultOpen={false}>

**The situation:** The customer mentions they are evaluating a competitor or have been approached by one.

**Do not:** Panic, badmouth the competitor, or immediately offer discounts.

**Do:**
1. **Stay calm and curious:** "That is helpful to know. What prompted the evaluation? Is there something specific they are offering that interests you?"
2. **Understand the gap:** Is this about price, features, service, or just a procurement process requirement?
3. **Quantify switching costs:** "Migrating your identity graph, re-implementing integrations, and retraining your team typically takes 6-9 months. During that time, your marketing operations are disrupted."
4. **Reinforce value delivered:** Point back to your ROI slide. "You are generating $X in value today. What is the risk of losing that during a transition?"
5. **Offer to address gaps:** If there is a genuine feature gap, share roadmap plans. If it is about price, loop in your account executive for commercial discussion.
6. **Escalate internally:** After the QBR, brief your manager and account executive on the competitive threat. Develop a joint retention strategy.

</Expandable>

### Executive Summary Template

Use this template for the written executive summary you send before and after the QBR.

>
> **Overall Health: [Green/Yellow/Red]**
>
> **Key Highlights:**
> - [Highlight 1: e.g., "2.4M unique profiles activated across 6 destinations"]
> - [Highlight 2: e.g., "Campaign ROAS improved 32% vs. pre-Zeotap baseline"]

### Final Checklist

Use this checklist before every QBR to ensure nothing is missed.

**5 days out:**
- [ ] Platform usage data pulled and analyzed
- [ ] Health score reviewed and dimension breakdown documented
- [ ] Support ticket summary prepared
- [ ] ROI metrics calculated
- [ ] Previous QBR action items reviewed

**3 days out:**
- [ ] Deck built with all 10 slides
- [ ] Narrative rehearsed (can you present without reading slides?)
- [ ] Internal review with manager completed
- [ ] Expansion opportunity identified and positioned in recommendations slide

**1 day out:**
- [ ] Agenda sent to customer with executive summary
- [ ] Calendar confirmed with all attendees
- [ ] Backup plan if executive sponsor drops (condensed agenda for remaining attendees)

**Day of:**
- [ ] Join 5 minutes early to test screen share and audio
- [ ] Record the meeting (with permission) for internal reference
- [ ] Take notes on action items in real time

**Day after:**
- [ ] Follow-up email sent within 24 hours
- [ ] Action items logged in CRM with owners and due dates
- [ ] Internal action plan created
- [ ] Next QBR scheduled
